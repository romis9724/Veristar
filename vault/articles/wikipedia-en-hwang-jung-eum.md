**Hwang Jung-eum** (; born December 25, 1984) is a South Korean actress and singer. She first gained recognition for her role in the sitcom *High Kick Through the Roof* (2009). After starring in her first leading role in television series *Listen to My Heart* (2011), she became notable for *Full House Take 2* (2012), *Secret Love* (2013), *Kill Me, Heal Me* (2015), *She Was Pretty* (2015), *The Undateables* (2018), *Mystic Pop-up Bar* (2020), and *The Escape of the Seven* (2023–2024).

##  Career 
### 2001–2008: Career beginnings
Hwang Jung-eum debuted with K-pop girl group Sugar in 2001 as a lead vocalist, but left the group in 2004 to pursue a solo career.<ref></ref><ref></ref> Hwang appeared as a recurring guest on the variety show *Love Letter* from 2004 to 2006, in which her onscreen pairing with singer Kim Jong-min became popular. She officially made her acting debut in the television drama *The Person I Love* in 2007.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

### 2009–2014: Rising popularity as a television actress
In 2009, she and real-life boyfriend Kim Yong-jun (of boy band SG Wannabe) joined the second season of reality dating show *We Got Married* as the first real-life couple to be featured on the show.<ref></ref> Later that year, Hwang rose to mainstream stardom when she appeared in the daily sitcom *High Kick Through the Roof*.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref>

After her role on *High Kick*, Hwang signed numerous advertising contracts. In several interviews, she recounts how she only had  in her bank account when she started the show, but walked away with  when it ended.<ref></ref><ref></ref> She was ranked 18th on *Forbes Korea's* Top 40 Celebrities in 2011,<ref></ref> and was among the Top 7 Female Commercial Stars in Korea, as compiled by *Ilgan Sports*.<ref></ref>

In February, 2010, Hwang hosted MBC's *Star Dance Battle* alongside Kim Shin-young, Oh Sang-jin, and Shin Bong-sun. Later that year in December, she was one of the co-hosts of the annual music program *SBS Gayo Daejeon* with Super Junior's Kim Hee-chul, CNBLUE's Jung Yong-hwa, and 2AM's Jo Kwon.<ref></ref>

Hwang then began to commit to acting in television series.<ref name=journey /> Between 2010 and 2012, she starred in the 1970s historical epic *Giant*,<ref></ref><ref></ref> melodrama romance *Listen to My Heart*,<ref></ref> medical drama *Golden Time*, and romantic comedy *Full House Take 2*. *Listen to My Heart* marked Hwang's first leading role.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref>

In 2013, Hwang was cast in crime comedy *Incarnation of Money*.<ref name=Advises /><ref></ref> In the same year, she starred in the hit melodrama crime series *Secret Love*, which achieved high TV viewership ratings. The drama resulted in Hwang winning multiple awards at the 2013 KBS Drama Awards.<ref></ref><ref></ref> The following year she led the show *Endless Love*, a period drama set in the 1980s–1990s.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

### 2015–present: Continued success
In 2015, Hwang reunited with some of her former costars, namely Ji Sung from *Secret Love*, for the romantic comedy series *Kill Me, Heal Me*;<ref></ref><ref name=candidly></ref> then with Park Seo-joon from *Kill Me, Heal Me* and Go Joon-hee from *Listen to My Heart* for another romantic comedy, *She Was Pretty*.<ref></ref><ref></ref> Both dramas were commercial successes and solidified her popularity outside South Korea, in countries like China.<ref></ref> *Ize* magazine named her as one of the 10 Persons of the Year<ref></ref> and Hwang was selected as Gallup Korea's Television Actor of the Year in 2015.<ref></ref> The two MBC series won Hwang multiple awards at the 2015 MBC Drama Awards.<ref></ref>

Hwang was cast in her first big screen leading role in the 2015 romantic comedy film *My Sister, the Pig Lady*,<ref></ref><ref></ref> which was screened at the 39th Montreal World Film Festival as part of their *World Greats* selection.<ref></ref> The film also won Grand Prize at the 2016 Osaka Asian Film Festival.<ref></ref>

Less than a month after her marriage, Hwang was cast in the 2016 romantic comedy series, *Lucky Romance*.<ref></ref> She then reunited with Namkoong Min in the 2018 romantic comedy *The Undateables*, with whom she had starred alongside seven years ago in *Listen to My Heart*.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

In 2020, Hwang was cast in two drama series – fantasy mystery *Mystic Pop-up Bar*<ref></ref> and romantic comedy *Men Are Men*.<ref></ref>

In 2022, Hwang accepted an offer to star in well-known screenwriter Kim Soon-ok's upcoming 2023 SBS crime mystery drama *The Escape of the Seven*.<ref></ref>

In July 2023, Hwang signed with a new agency, Y.ONE Entertainment.<ref></ref>

In 2025, Hwang admitted to embezzling more than 4.34 billion won (US$2.86 million) from a company that she owned to invest in cryptocurrency.<ref></ref> She was subsequently sentenced to a two year prison term suspended for four years by the Jeju District Court.<ref></ref>

##  Personal life 
###  Relationships 
Hwang dated Kim Yong-jun for nine years.<ref></ref><ref></ref> They first met in 2005, when Hwang starred in the SG Wannabe music video for *My Heart's Treasure Box*.<ref></ref> The couple then publicly confirmed their relationship in January 2008. *Yonhap News Agency* broke the news on May 15, 2015, that the couple had ended their relationship after she wrapped up filming for *Kill Me, Heal Me*.<ref></ref> Hwang later confirmed this in an interview with KBS2's *Entertainment Relay*.<ref></ref>

On December 8, 2015, Hwang's agency, C-JeS Entertainment, confirmed that she had been dating Lee Young-don, a professional golfer and businessman, for the past four months.<ref></ref> On January 7, 2016, Hwang announced her upcoming marriage to Lee Young-don, with the wedding ceremony to be held in February.<ref></ref><ref></ref> On February 26, 2016, the wedding reception was held at Hotel Shilla, Seoul.<ref></ref>

In February, 2017, Hwang was confirmed to be four months pregnant with her first child<ref></ref> and gave birth to a son on August 15, 2017.<ref></ref>

On September 3, 2020, after four years of marriage, Hwang's agency C-JeS Entertainment announced that Hwang had submitted an application for divorce and would start the divorce mediation process.<ref></ref>

On July 9, 2021, her agency, C-JeS Entertainment, announced that Hwang and her husband had resolved their conflict during their divorce mediation and that the two had decided to remain as a married couple.<ref></ref>

On October 12, 2021, Hwang's agency announced that she was pregnant with her second child and expected to give birth the following year.<ref></ref> On March 16, 2022, she gave birth to a second son.<ref></ref>

On February 22, 2024, it was confirmed that Hwang had filed for divorce from her husband for a second time.<ref></ref> Their divorce was finalized on May 26, 2025.<ref></ref>

On July 22, 2024, her agency confirmed that she was dating basketball player Kim Jong-kyu.<ref></ref> On August 5, 2024, two weeks after the public confirmation of their relationship, Hwang's agency confirmed in a short statement that the two had broken-up.<ref></ref>
