**Kim Yoon-ji** (; born March 16, 1989), known professionally as **Jung So-min** (), is a South Korean actress. Jung made her screen debut in 2010 with a supporting role in the television series *Bad Guy*. She then got her first leading role that same year in the romantic comedy television series *Playful Kiss*. She is known for her leading roles in the television series *Because This Is My First Life* (2017), *Alchemy of Souls* (2022), *Love Next Door* (2024), and *Would You Marry Me?* (2025), as well as the film *Love Reset* (2023).

## Career
### 2010–2015: Beginnings

Jung first attracted attention when she made her acting debut in 2010 with a supporting role in the television series *Bad Guy*.<ref></ref><ref></ref> This led to a leading role in her next project *Playful Kiss*, the Korean adaptation of the popular manga *Itazura na Kiss*.<ref name=":0"></ref> Though it received low ratings in Korea, the romantic comedy was popular overseas, further raising Jung's profile.<ref></ref>

Jung then took a short break in 2011 to concentrate on her studies at the Korea National University of Arts.<ref></ref> In 2012, she returned to the small screen as part of the ensemble cast of the sitcom *Standby*.<ref name=":1"></ref> Later that year, she played a more mature role opposite Sung Joon in the cable series *Can We Get Married?*.<ref></ref><ref name=":2"></ref>

Jung left her talent agency Bloom Entertainment in 2013 and joined SM Culture & Contents.<ref></ref> She then appeared in *Came to Me and Became a Star*, a single-episode anthology *Drama Special*.<ref></ref>

In 2014, Jung played a self-centered heiress who falls for the protagonist (played by Kang Ji-hwan) in *Big Man*.<ref></ref><ref name=":3"></ref> This was followed in 2015 by a supporting role in coming-of-age film *Twenty*,<ref name=":4"></ref> and leading roles in horror-romance film *Alice: Boy from Wonderland* and disaster-medical drama *D-Day*.<ref name=":5"></ref><ref name=":6"></ref>

### 2016–present: Leading roles in film and television
Jung starred in a Drama Special *The Red Teacher* in 2016.<ref name="rt"></ref> She was then cast as the female lead in *The Sound of Your Heart* opposite Lee Kwang-soo in December of the same year.<ref name=":7"></ref> The web drama was a success in China and gained more than 100 million views on Sohu.<ref></ref>

In 2017, Jung was cast in KBS weekend family drama *My Father is Strange*.<ref name=":8"></ref> She also starred in the body-swap comedy *Daddy You, Daughter Me* opposite veteran actor Yoon Je-moon.<ref name=":9"></ref> She then left SM Culture & Contents in June and signed with new management agency Jellyfish Entertainment.<ref></ref><ref></ref> In August, she confirmed her appearance in tvN's romantic comedy series *Because This Is My First Life* alongside Lee Min-ki.<ref name=":10"></ref><ref></ref> She also released an OST "Because You Are Beside Me" for the drama.<ref name="byh"></ref>

Jung was chosen to play the lead role in *The Smile Has Left Your Eyes* in 2018. This is an adaptation of the 2002 Japanese television series *Sora Kara Furu Ichioku no Hoshi*<ref name=":11"></ref><ref></ref> where she also recorded an OST "Star" with her co-star, Seo In-guk.<ref name="star"></ref> The same year, she was cast in the historical comedy flick *Homme Fatale*.<ref name=":12"></ref><ref></ref> She wrapped up 2018 with her first radio show *Jung So Min's Young Street* on SBS Power FM, thus achieving her dream to become a radio DJ someday.<ref name="ys"></ref>

Jung left Jellyfish Entertainment in August 2019 and signed with another management company Blossom Entertainment.<ref></ref> She then became a cast member in a healing variety program *Little Forest*,<ref name="lf"></ref> which is designed as a home kids garden development project for children. In December, she stepped down from her DJ post in *Jung So Min's Young Street*.

In 2020, Jung starred in the medical drama *Soul Mechanic* alongside Shin Ha-kyun<ref name=":13"></ref> where she portrayed a musical rising star with terrible anger management issues.

In 2021, she did a cameo in Park Joon-hwa's fantasy romantic comedy *My Roommate Is a Gumiho*, appearing as the woman whom protagonist Jang Ki-yong loved in the past.<ref></ref> She also appeared in TV romance drama *Monthly Magazine Home* opposite Kim Ji-seok as an editor of a lifestyle magazine *Monthly House*.<ref name="mk:c1"></ref>

In 2022, Jung returned to the small screen with the tvN period fantasy drama *Alchemy of Souls* where she played a skilled assassin whose soul is trapped inside the body of servant girl, alongside Lee Jae-wook.<ref></ref> However, she only made a cameo in the season 2.<ref></ref> In August, Jung signed with TH Company.<ref></ref> In September, Jung returned to the big screen for the first time in three years with the sci-fi film *Project Wolf Hunting*.<ref></ref>

In 2023, Jung signed with Ieum Hashtag.<ref></ref> In October of the same year, Jung starred in the South Korean romantic comedy film, *Love Reset*, directed by Nam Dae-jung, co-starring Kang Ha-neul where she played a film producer who suffering from amnesia due to an unexpected accident.

Jung accepted the offer to star alongside Jung Hae-in in 2024 tvN romantic comedy drama, *Love Next Door* helmed by director Yoo Je-won and writer Shin Ha-eun of *Hometown Cha-Cha-Cha* where she portrayed a character of a woman who is successful in life but suddenly breaks down one day and returns to her hometown to reboot her life.<ref></ref> The drama dominated the ratings for a number of weeks.<ref></ref> In 2025, she starred in SBS TV's romantic comedy *Would You Marry Me?*.<ref></ref>

In December 2025, Jung signed with Hiin Entertainment.<ref></ref>
