**Jung Yu-mi** (, born January 18, 1983) is a South Korean actress. Known for her screen presence and versatility,<ref></ref> she has received numerous accolades including a Baeksang Arts Award, two Blue Dragon Film Awards, and a Grand Bell Award. Jung made her feature film debut in *Blossom Again* (2005) and has since starred in the critically acclaimed films *Family Ties* (2006), *Chaw* (2009), *My Dear Desperado* (2010), and *Sleep* (2023), as well as box office hits *Silenced* (2011), *Train to Busan* (2016), and *Kim Ji-young: Born 1982* (2019). She has also appeared in director Hong Sang-soo's films, notably *Oki's Movie* (2010) and *Our Sunhi* (2013). On television, she has starred in the series *Live* (2018) and *Love Your Enemy* (2024).

## Career
Jung Yu-mi made her acting debut in short films, notably *How to Operate a Polaroid Camera*.<ref></ref> Shortly after, the then-aspiring actress impressed critics in the feature film *Blossom Again*, in which she gave an engrossing performance as an emotionally vulnerable teenager experiencing her first love,<ref></ref> and thus received several newcomer awards that year.<ref></ref> Her next film *Family Ties* was also critically acclaimed,<ref></ref> for which she won Best Supporting Actress at the Blue Dragon Film Awards.<ref><br></ref>

Jung's filmography is a diverse mix of arthouse indies such as *The Room Nearby*<ref></ref> and *Cafe Noir*,<ref></ref> cult monster flick *Chaw*,<ref></ref> odd-couple romantic comedy *My Dear Desperado*,<ref></ref> and melodrama *Come, Closer*.<ref></ref> *The Crucible* (also known as *Silenced*), inspired by true events about a group of handicapped children who suffer physical and sexual abuse at the hands of their teachers, drew nearly 5 million viewers and became one of the highest-grossing films that Jung has starred in.<ref><br><br></ref> Her ability to portray the lives of ordinary people in ways that are not ordinary has made her one of the most sought-after actresses for auteurial films, as evidenced by her continued collaboration with director Hong Sang-soo.<ref></ref> She played the titular character in Hong's *Oki's Movie* (2010) and *Our Sunhi* (2013), where her fragile appeal is underpinned by a quiet strength and straightforwardness.<ref><br><br></ref> *Oki's Movie* premiered at the 67th Venice Film Festival, while *Our Sunhi* premiered at the 66th Locarno International Film Festival. In 2015, she starred in the female-centric indie film *The Table* directed by Kim Jong-kwan, which premiered at the 21st Busan International Film Festival.<ref></ref>*]]Jung has also starred in several commercial films, such as *Manhole* (2014), where she plays a killer.<ref></ref> She was cast in the zombie thriller *Train to Busan* (2016) directed by Yeon Sang-ho, which premiered at the Cannes Film Festival.<ref><br></ref> The film was a major success, surpassing 10 million audiences.<ref></ref> Jung reunited with Yeon in another film, *Psychokinesis*, a black comedy which premiered in 2018.<ref></ref> Though much less prolific in television, Jung uses her quirky, offbeat image to great effect when playing peculiar but adorable characters in *Que Sera Sera* (2007), *I Need Romance 2012*, and *Discovery of Love* (2014).<ref><br><br><br></ref> In 2018, Jung starred in the tvN drama *Live'', written by Noh Hee-kyung where she played a police officer.<ref><br></ref>

Jung also showcased her sweet singing voice through the song "Andromeda" with Sung Si-kyung in 2016.<ref></ref> They later did a live duet of the song in *You Hee-yeol's Sketchbook* in 2017. In 2019, Jung starred in the feminist film *Kim Ji-young: Born 1982* based on the best-selling novel of the same title.<ref></ref> The film was released in October 2019.<ref></ref> In 2020, Jung played the titular role in Netflix's series *The School Nurse Files*.<ref></ref>
