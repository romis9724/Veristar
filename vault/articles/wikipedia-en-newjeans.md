**NewJeans<!-- DO NOT change to NJZ as per talk page consensus -->** () is a South Korean girl group formed by ADOR, a sub-label of Hybe. The group debuted with five members: Minji, Hanni, Danielle, Haerin, and Hyein. Formerly produced by Min Hee-jin, they are known for their girl next door image and musical stylings reminiscent of the 1990s and 2000s.

The group released their first single "Attention" on July 22, 2022. It was followed shortly afterwards by two other singles, "Hype Boy" and "Cookie", with "Hype Boy" becoming the longest-running song on the *Billboard* Global 200 by a K-pop female act. The singles were all featured on their eponymous debut extended play (EP), released in August 2022. In January 2023, they released their first single album, *OMG*, to commercial success. It was accompanied by two singles, "Ditto" and "OMG". "Ditto" gained widespread popularity, becoming the longest-running number-one song on the Circle Digital Chart and the group's first entry on both the *Billboard* Hot 100 and the UK Singles Chart.

Their second EP, *Get Up*, peaked at number one on the US *Billboard* 200 and sold over one million copies in South Korea. Its lead single, "Super Shy", became the group's highest-charting single on the *Billboard* Global 200 (number two), the US *Billboard* Hot 100, and the UK Singles Chart. NewJeans has received rookie awards and was featured in listicles such as *Time* Next Generation Leaders and *Forbes* Korea Power Celebrity 40. IFPI named NewJeans the eighth best-selling artist worldwide in 2023.

In 2024, a dispute over NewJeans' management arose as former ADOR CEO Min Hee-jin and the members clashed with Hybe and ADOR executives. The members sought to terminate their contract, which ADOR denied. In March 2025, the Seoul Central District Court granted an injunction filed by ADOR, blocking the group from carrying out independent activities. The group subsequently announced a hiatus later that month. The court ruled in favor of ADOR on October 30, which resulted in Hyein and Haerin agreeing to return to the label. On December 29, it was announced that Hanni had also agreed to return, while Danielle's contract was terminated. Discussion regarding Minji's contract is ongoing.

## Name
The group's name "NewJeans" alludes to the idea that jeans are a timeless fashion item and the group's intention to carve a timeless image for themselves.<ref name=":1"></ref> The name is also a word play on the phrase "new genes", referring to the group ushering a new generation of pop music.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

In February 2025, the members changed their name to "NJZ" amid their contract dispute with Hybe.<ref name="CNN"></ref> The group stopped using "NJZ" two months later, following an injunction.<ref name="NJZ"></ref>

## History
### 2011–2022: Pre-debut activities and formation
Before debuting with NewJeans, several group members were involved in television, music and dance. When Australian-born Danielle Marsh lived in South Korea, she was a regular cast member of tvN's *Rainbow Kindergarten*, a variety show that aired until 2011.<ref></ref> She also appeared in the TV shows *Jesse's Play Kitchen* and *My Heart's Crayon*.<ref></ref> Hyein debuted as a member of the children's music group U.SSO Girl alongside Rora of Babymonster in November 2017 under the stage name U.Jeong,<ref name="Yahoo"></ref><ref></ref> before departing from the group one year later.<ref name="debut1" /> In December 2020, she joined the music group and YouTube collective Play With Me Club formed by PocketTV, and graduated from the group on May 3, 2021.<ref></ref> Vietnamese-Australian Hanni Pham began performing in Melbourne as a member of the Aemina Dance Crew who covered choreographies of K-pop groups.<ref></ref>

Preparations for a new girl group in collaboration between Hybe and Source Music began as early as 2019<ref></ref><ref name="BeAttitude1"></ref> where Min Hee-jin was to be the creative director, after joining as the chief brand officer that same year. Min was widely recognized for her art direction as visual director at SM Entertainment.<ref></ref> At the time of Min joining in 2019, it was revealed she would be the creative lead in multiple girl groups, excluding Big Hit, while a separate label would also be established later where she would be a producer.<ref></ref> Global auditions took place between September and October 2019.<ref name="BeAttitude1" /><ref></ref> Minji, a Source Music trainee, was featured in the promotional videos for these auditions. Various other auditions were held, and it was later revealed Hanni was the only trainee from the Plus Global Auditions to join NewJeans. Other members came from various backgrounds: Danielle was a former YG Entertainment trainee, passing a separate Source Music audition in July 2020 while Haerin was a trainee at another agency and was street cast at the end of 2019, officially joining Source in early 2020.<ref></ref> Hanni and Minji made a guest appearance in BTS's 2021 music video for "Permission to Dance".<ref></ref> All of the members then transferred to ADOR at the end of 2021.<ref></ref>

In late 2021, it was announced that the project moved to Hybe's newly established independent label ADOR, after Min was appointed the label's CEO.<ref></ref> A second round of global auditions were held between December 2021 and January 2022,<ref></ref> and the group's line-up was finalized in March 2022.<ref name="BeAttitude2" /><ref name="Q3"></ref> Dubbed "Min Hee-jin's Girl Group" by several media outlets,<ref name="BeAttitude2"></ref><ref></ref> the group was originally scheduled to launch in 2021 but was later postponed due to the COVID-19 pandemic.<ref name="BeAttitude1" />

### 2022–2023: Debut with *New Jeans*, and *OMG*

On July 1, 2022, ADOR teased the launch of their new girl group by posting three animated videos of the numbers "22", "7" and "22", fuelling speculation that content would be released on July 22.<ref></ref> That day, NewJeans surprise-released the music video for their debut single, "Attention", without any prior promotion or information on the group's lineup.<ref></ref> *Billboard* described the move as "risky-but-ultimately invigorating", crediting its success to "an emphasis on the music before anything else".<ref name="billboard"></ref> The video was followed by the announcement of their upcoming self-titled debut extended play, which would contain four songs including two additional singles.<ref name="debut1"></ref>

NewJeans released their second single, "Hype Boy", on July 23, 2022, along with a clip revealing the members' names and four music videos specific to their perspectives.<ref name="hurt"></ref> "Hype Boy" reached number two on the South Korean Circle Digital Chart,<ref></ref> while it became the longest-running song on the *Billboard* Global 200 by a K-pop female act,<ref></ref> charting for 42 weeks.<ref></ref> A music video for their b-side "Hurt" was released two days later.<ref name="hurt" /> Pre-orders for the EP surpassed 444,000 copies in three days.<ref></ref> NewJeans released their debut EP for download and streaming  on August 1, 2022, alongside its third single, "Cookie".<ref></ref> *Pitchfork* described *New Jeans* as a plush and stylish collection of '90s and '00s-indebted R&B.<ref></ref> "Cookie" was criticized by some reviewers, who thought the lyrics contained a sexual innuendo.<ref name=":1" /> ADOR denied the accusation and stated that the lyrics refer to "the paired idea of burning CDs and baking cookies, which share the same conceptual verb in Korean".<ref></ref> The physical version of the EP was released on August 8 and sold over one million copies, becoming the best selling debut album by a K-pop female act in South Korea and the only debut album to achieve this since Circle Chart's establishment in 2011.<ref name="koreaherald2"></ref> The group made their broadcast debut on Mnet's *M Countdown* on August 4, performing all three singles.<ref></ref> In October 2022, they held their first live performances overseas, appearing at KCON in Saudi Arabia and Japan.<ref></ref> At the end of the year, they won rookie awards at the Melon Music Awards, the Golden Disc Awards, the Korean Music Awards, the Seoul Music Awards, the Asia Artist Awards, and The Fact Music Awards.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

NewJeans released "Ditto" on December 19, 2022, as the first single from their first single album, *OMG*.<ref name="koreaherald"></ref> "Ditto" became the longest-running number-one song on the Circle Digital Chart, topping the chart for thirteen weeks.<ref></ref><ref></ref> It was NewJeans' first entry on both the *Billboard* Hot 100, peaking at number 82, and the UK Singles Chart, charting at number 95.<ref name="koreaherald" /> Later in 2023, "Ditto" won Song of the Year at the MAMA Awards, the Melon Music Awards, the Golden Disc Awards, and the Asia Artist Awards.<ref></ref> "Ditto" also held the record for longest-running Perfect all-kill—a feat referring to a song's consistent number-one position on IChart's real-time, daily, and weekly charts—at 655 hours, until Huntrix's "Golden" surpassed it in 2025.<ref></ref><ref></ref> *OMG* was released on January 2, 2023.<ref name="koreaherald" /> Reviewers commended the album for its retro-style theme.<ref name="joongang"></ref> It debuted at number one on the Circle Album Chart,<ref name="omg"></ref> which sold 700,000 copies in its first week of release.<ref name="joongang" /> It became their first album to sell over one million copies,<ref></ref> shortly before *New Jeans* also reached one million copies sold.<ref name="koreaherald2" /> *OMG* was accompanied by a second single of the same name, which went viral on TikTok, peaked at number 74 on the *Billboard* Hot 100,<ref></ref><ref></ref> and became the group's most streamed song on Spotify.<ref></ref> "OMG" won the Best Song at the 33rd Seoul Music Awards.<ref></ref>

### 2023–2024: *Get Up* and Japanese debut

In July 2023, NewJeans held their first sold-out fan meeting, titled Bunnies Camp, at the SK Olympic Handball Gymnasium in Seoul.<ref></ref> The group released their second EP, *Get Up*, on July 21, 2023.<ref></ref> *Get Up* became the group's first entry and number-one on the *Billboard* 200, making NewJeans the second K-pop girl group to top the chart after Blackpink.<ref name="hot100records"></ref> The EP reached number two on the Circle Album Chart and sold 1.65 million copies in its first week of release,<ref></ref><ref></ref> becoming NewJeans' third consecutive album to sell over one million copies.<ref></ref> It was supported by three singles: "Super Shy", "ETA", and "Cool with You".<ref></ref> All the singles entered the *Billboard* Hot 100, making NewJeans the first K-pop female artist to have three simultaneous entries on the chart.<ref name="hot100records" /> "Super Shy" topped the Circle Digital Chart and became their best-performing track on several international charts,<ref></ref><ref></ref> which led NewJeans to achieve their first number-one on the *Billboard* Emerging Artists chart.<ref></ref> To promote the EP, NewJeans held live performances in multiple countries and collaborated with global brands and celebrities.<ref></ref> In August 2023, they became the first K-pop girl group to perform at Lollapalooza with their first live performance in the United States.<ref></ref> That same month, NewJeans performed at the Summer Sonic Festival in Japan.<ref></ref>

Throughout 2023, NewJeans released five other singles. In collaboration with Coca-Cola, they released two singles: "Zero" in April and "Be Who You Are (Real Magic)", also featuring Jon Batiste, J.I.D, and Camilo, in May.<ref></ref><ref></ref> A remix of "Zero" featuring J.I.D was released in June.<ref></ref> NewJeans recorded "Beautiful Restriction" and "Our Night Is More Beautiful Than Your Day" for the soundtracks of the streaming television series *A Time Called You* (2023) and *My Demon* (2023–2024), respectively.<ref></ref><ref></ref> In October, the group collaborated with Riot Games to release "Gods" as the theme song for the 2023 *League of Legends* World Championship.<ref></ref> In December 2023, NewJeans released their first remix album, *NJWMX*, comprising songs from *New Jeans* and *OMG* remixed by South Korean producers 250 and FRNK.<ref></ref> At the end of the year, they became the first K-pop girl group to perform on *Dick Clark's New Year's Rockin' Eve*.<ref></ref>

Several publications named NewJeans among the most prominent music acts of 2023, citing their commercial success and influence in the music, advertising, and fashion industries.<ref name="advertising2"></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref><ref name="billboard1" /> They sold 4.39 million album copies, the most-ever for a K-pop female artist in a single year,<ref></ref> and broke the Guinness World Record for the fastest K-pop act to reach one billion streams on Spotify.<ref></ref> The International Federation of the Phonographic Industry (IFPI) ranked NewJeans at number eight on their list of the Global Recording Artist of the Year, making them the second highest-ranked female artist of 2023.<ref></ref> Along with Lim Young-woong, the group topped Gallup Korea's Singer of the Year poll.<ref></ref> At the MAMA Awards, NewJeans became the first girl group in twelve years to win Artist of the Year and the third girl group in history to receive the award, following 2NE1 in 2010 and Girls' Generation in 2011.<ref></ref> After also winning Artist of the Year at the Melon Music Awards, they became the first female artist to win both awards in the same year.<ref></ref> At the *Billboard* Women in Music, they became the first K-pop group to win Group of the Year.<ref></ref> NewJeans became the first foreign artist in history to win the Excellent Work Award and be nominated for the Grand Prix with "Ditto" at the Japan Record Awards.<ref></ref>

In March 2024, NewJeans announced they would release two singles in May and June.<ref name="announcement"></ref> "How Sweet" was released on May 24, along with the B-side track "Bubble Gum". "Supernatural" was released on June 21, along with the B-side track "Right Now". The release of "Supernatural" and "Right Now" marked the group's official Japanese debut, and after its release they began their first cycle of promotional activities in Japan, including a fan meeting, titled Bunnies Camp 2024 Tokyo Dome, at the Tokyo Dome on June 26–27.<ref name="announcement" /> It was also announced that NewJeans had plans to release an album in the second half of 2024,<ref name="announcement" /> however the album plans were later halted.<ref name="delay"></ref><ref></ref> On November 16, NewJeans won the Grand Artist Award at the inaugural Korea Grand Music Awards.<ref></ref>

### 2024–present: Conflict with Hybe, hiatus, and Danielle's contract termination
In April 2024, a shareholders' dispute broke out between Hybe Corporation's top executives and NewJeans' CEO, Min Hee-jin. Hybe executives claimed Min had plotted to gain full ownership of the group, while Min accused Hybe of subjecting NewJeans to unfair competition from its other girl groups.<ref></ref> The dispute led to Min's dismissal as CEO of ADOR on August 27.<ref></ref>

Throughout the dispute, the NewJeans members publicly supported Min, including a surprise YouTube livestream on September 11, during which they expressed distrust of Hybe's management,  and detailed issues such as workplace harassment, leaking of private information, and the deletion of previously released content.<ref></ref><ref></ref> They ended the broadcast by giving Hybe chairman Bang Si-hyuk an ultimatum to reinstate Min by September 25.<ref></ref> Jon Caramanica of *The New York Times* described the conflict as a significant and rare labor dispute in K-pop, calling it one of the most high-profile in recent years.<ref></ref> On September 25, Hybe rejected NewJeans' ultimatum to reinstate Min, citing a company policy of separating management from production, but confirmed that Min would remain the group's producer.<ref></ref> Despite the conflict, Min affirmed her commitment to NewJeans in an interview with TV Asahi, stating that she had a plan to overcome the challenges.<ref></ref> Min resigned from Hybe in November.<ref></ref>

On October 15, Hanni testified as a reference witness before the National Assembly's Environment and Labor Committee, which was conducting an audit focused on workplace harassment and artist protection in the entertainment industry. Hanni accused Hybe and senior managers of undermining NewJeans and described the discrimination her group faced. ADOR's CEO, Kim Joo-young, also testified and pledged cooperation with the investigation.<ref></ref><ref></ref> The case was later dismissed by the government agency, citing that K-pop group members are not classified as workers and are not entitled to labor rights.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

On November 13, *Yonhap News* reported that the NewJeans members had sent a letter to ADOR giving them an ultimatum that if major breaches were not remedied within 14 days, they would terminate their exclusive contracts. This action followed a leaked internal document suggesting Hybe intended to discard NewJeans and after their request to reinstate Min as ADOR's CEO was rejected.<ref></ref> On November 28, the five members announced the termination of their exclusive contract with ADOR, citing violations of clauses including artist protection.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref> On November 29, the members pledged to pursue activities independently and fight for rights to the group's name.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref> ADOR denied any contract breaches and stated the members remained under contractual obligations.<ref></ref> The Korea Management Federation (KMF) and Korea Entertainment Producers' Association (KEPA) sided with ADOR, arguing that unilateral contract terminations harm industry principles and investments.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref>

On December 5, ADOR petitioned the court to confirm the validity of NewJeans' contract.<ref></ref><ref></ref> On December 14, the five NewJeans members launched a new Instagram account, "jeanzforfree", separate from the official account managed by ADOR.<ref name=":0"></ref> Their first post shared details about offering free food and beverages to K-pop fans attending a rally in Yeouido, Seoul, supporting the impeachment of South Korean president Yoon Suk Yeol.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref> On January 13, 2025, ADOR filed an injunction with the Seoul Central District Court to prevent the members from independently signing advertising contracts while their contract dispute remained unresolved.<ref></ref> On February 7, the members announced a new group name, "NJZ",<ref></ref> and were announced as headliners for Hong Kong music festival ComplexCon on March 23 under the new name.<ref name="CNN" /> Two days before the performance, ADOR obtained a preliminary injunction barring the group from engaging in independent activities. The group proceeded to perform at the music festival as planned,<ref></ref> performing an unreleased song titled "Pit Stop" and a series of covers, and concluding with an announcement of a pause of all activities for the foreseeable future.<ref></ref> 

The group filed an objection to the injunction,<ref></ref> which was upheld in favor of ADOR in April. The group then filed an appeal to the Seoul High Court and stopped using the "NJZ" name.<ref></ref><ref name="NJZ"/> On May 30, the same District Court ordered each member to pay ADOR  for every unauthorized activity performed from that date onward.<ref></ref> On June 18, the High Court denied the group's appeal,<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref> with ADOR releasing a statement hoping the members would return "to where they belong".<ref></ref> On August 14 and September 11, the Seoul Central District Court held two mediation sessions between NewJeans and ADOR, both of which concluded without agreement. On October 30, the court ruled in favor of ADOR, upholding the validity of the contracts until 2029.<ref></ref><ref></ref> The members said they would appeal the ruling,<ref></ref> but on November 12, ADOR announced that Haerin and Hyein had agreed to return to work with the label.<ref></ref> The remaining three members then announced their intent to return, with ADOR stating they were "confirming the sincerity of [their intentions]."<ref></ref><ref></ref> On December 29, ADOR announced that an agreement had been reached for Hanni to return to the company, and that the exclusive contract with Danielle was terminated,<ref></ref> in the following day suing her, and other close collaborators, for ₩43 billion (US$29.8 million) in damages.<ref></ref> Minji's contract discussions are still ongoing.<ref></ref><ref></ref> As of May 7, 2026, talks are still ongoing, with them being described as progressing "positively" by ADOR.<ref></ref>

## Artistry
### Musical style and lyrics
NewJeans' music spans genres such as R&B, electropop, and hip hop.<ref></ref> It is characterized by mellow beats, atmospheric synthesizers, and live vocals.<ref name="guardian" /><ref></ref><ref name="Thompson-2023" /> Their debut EP and first single album *OMG* are predominantly midtempo pop and R&B that evoke music of the 1990s and 2000s decades.<ref name="vogue"></ref><ref name="koreaherald1"></ref> There were comparisons to the sound of late-1990s K-pop girl groups.<ref name="Thompson-2023" /><ref name="Choi-2023"></ref><ref name="nme"></ref> Music critics also identified elements of late-1990s and mid-2000s electronic and dance styles such as UK garage,<ref name="Thompson-2023" /><ref></ref> Baltimore club,<ref></ref> Jersey club,<ref name="Kim-2022"></ref> and moombahton.<ref name="nme" /> With their second EP *Get Up*, they ventured more into dance and club music with a more rhythmic production expanding on the group's UK garage-influenced past releases.<ref name="Thompson-2023"></ref> Meanwhile, with their "Supernatural" release, they explored new jack swing and dance-pop with 250's interpolation of "Back of My Mind", a 2009 song by Japanese singer Manami which was produced by Pharrell Williams.<ref></ref> The *Hankook Ilbo* aligned NewJeans' style with the "newtro" aesthetic due to the retro sensibilities;<ref></ref> the newspaper's music critic Choi Eun-soo characterized their sound as "easy listening" devoid of "explosive" EDM embellishments that had saturated the market.<ref name="Choi-2023" /> Some critics, such as Sheldon Pearce and Joshua Minsoo Kim writing for NPR, described NewJeans' music as soft, contained, and delicate, which is a stark contrast to their contemporaries' "maximalist" and "harsh, buzzing" sounds.<ref name="Thompson-2023" /><ref name="Kim-2022" /> *The Guardian* Laura Snapes described their songs as "sleek and lethally hooky, yet playful and teeming with retro synths and experimental flourishes".<ref name="guardian"></ref> Writing in *The Korea Times*, scholar and cultural critic David Tizzard says that a lot of NewJeans' songs are influenced by 90s British jungle and drum and bass. In particular, their 2024 track "Right Now" is "not classic jungle or drum and bass because it's more polished and eschews the grittier samples. This is catchy: a sleek earworm. Rather than being from the LTJ Bukem wheelhouse of London, the track is closer to something from DJ Marky or the Sambass subgenre."<ref></ref>

NewJeans' music is mainly produced by South Korean singers 250 and Park Jin-su (also known as FRNK), while the members often participate in songwriting.<ref name="guardian" /> The members have talked about being inspired by film and television, in particular by "high school teen dramas" like *Clueless* (1995), *Mean Girls* (2004), and *The Princess Diaries* (2001).<ref></ref><ref></ref> Executive producer Min Hee-jin selects all NewJeans' songs and is responsible for the recording process.<ref name="interview1"></ref> Regarding the group's debut EP, Min said she "wanted to break the tacit formula of K-pop and make an album with the music I want".<ref></ref> Min disliked "high-pitched parts, awkward raps that suddenly appear, and singing methods that feel uniform" and chose to record without guide vocals, allowing the members to develop their own singing styles.<ref name="interview1" /><ref name="koreaherald1" /> Pearce argued that NewJeans' frequent collaborations with certain producers brought forth a cohesive discography with distinct identity and aesthetic.<ref name="Thompson-2023" />

### Image and fashion
NewJeans has influenced fashion and make-up trends with their style, developed by Choi Yu-mi. Having previously met Min while working with Shinee, Choi began working with Min and NewJeans in 2020, despite never having styled a girl group.<ref name="vogueuk"></ref><ref name="wkorea"></ref> Min stated that she wanted NewJeans' image to be "cool, chill, and sophisticated".<ref></ref> NewJeans debuted with a Y2K-inspired style that established them as "a symbol" of the Y2K fashion revival in South Korea.<ref></ref> The members wore "drastically" different outfits in the music videos to confuse fans, prompting them to rewatch the videos to identify the members.<ref></ref> For their hair and make-up, NewJeans worked with hairstylist Gi-hee and make-up artist Lee Nak-yeum, who developed a signature "natural" look for the group.<ref></ref><ref></ref> Azrin Tan argued in *Vogue Singapore* that NewJeans presented a "new aesthetic", characterized by minimal soft make-up and straight black hair, that defied beauty standards in the contemporary K-pop industry with its simplicity.<ref name="voguesingapore"></ref> Their styling contributed in shaping NewJeans' girl-next-door image,<ref name="cnn" /> deemed by Tan a "stark shift away from the usually high-octane aesthetic" of contemporary K-pop groups.<ref name="voguesingapore" />

NewJeans branched out and showcased a variety of concepts in the music videos released for *Get Up*, including cheerleading and ballet-inspired outfits.<ref></ref><ref></ref> Since NewJeans' debut, Choi has incorporated in their style elements from several modern trends, such as gorpcore and blokecore, and designers, such as Kiko Kostadinov, Martine Rose, Marine Serre, and Hyein Seo.<ref name="vogueuk" /><ref name="wkorea" /> Edith Winslow of *Vogue Australia* has described NewJeans' fashion style as a combination of "bold pieces, contemporary designers, and the confidence to pull it all off".<ref name="vogueau"></ref> *The New York Times* named them among the most stylish people of 2023.<ref></ref> In interviews, NewJeans have said they enjoy exploring different styles and expressing themselves through fashion.<ref></ref><ref></ref> The members' personal styles have also become influential, with Julian Bartolome of *Inquirer Super* saying they have "established themselves as modern-teen style icons".<ref></ref><ref></ref> All of the members have served as ambassadors for fashion and beauty brands: Hanni for Gucci, Armani Beauty, and UGG, Hyein for Louis Vuitton, Danielle for Burberry, YSL Beauty, and Celine, Minji for Chanel Korea, and Haerin for Dior.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

### Philanthropy
In December 2022, it was announced that NewJeans and ADOR would annually donate part of the profits from the group's album sales to the Snail of Love charity to fund cochlear implant surgery and speech therapy treatment for the hearing-impaired.<ref></ref> The following year, ADOR and NewJeans joined the Snail of Love's Soul Leader club, having donated a total of over  million.<ref></ref> In February 2023, NewJeans and ADOR donated  million to the World Food Programme to help victims of the earthquake that struck Turkey and Syria that month.<ref></ref> In May 2024, NewJeans donated all proceeds from their performances at seven university festivals to the Korea Scholarship Foundation.<ref></ref> In December 2024, the five members donated in their own name 100 million won, approximately $69,000, to children's charity ChildFund Korea.<ref></ref>
