**Han Ji-min** (; born November 5, 1982) is a South Korean actress. After minor roles in *All In* (2003) and *Dae Jang Geum* (2003–2004), Han had her breakout role in revenge series *Resurrection* (2005). This was followed by leading roles in period dramas *Capital Scandal* (2007) and *Yi San* (2007–2008), contemporary dramas *Cain and Abel* (2009) and *Padam Padam* (2011–2012), romantic dramas *Rooftop Prince* (2012), *Familiar Wife* (2018) ,and *The Light in Your Eyes* (2019), as well as drama film *Miss Baek* (2018) for which won Best Actress at the 55th Baeksang Arts Awards. Han has since starred in the series *Our Blues* (2022), *Behind Your Touch* (2023), and *Love Scout* (2025)

## Career
### 2003–2010: Beginnings and breakthrough
Han Ji-min started her career in show business while still a high school student. She made various commercial films (CF) and music videos before gaining wider attention in 2003, when she appeared in hit Korean television dramas *All In* and *Dae Jang Geum*.<ref></ref> Han said she hadn't dreamed of becoming a serious actress, but changed her mind after starring in *All In*, where she played the teenage counterpart of the drama's main character, played by popular actress Song Hye-kyo.

Han achieved a breakthrough with her performance in the critically acclaimed mania drama *Resurrection* in 2005.<ref>"Han Ji-min: I am a Happy Woman Addicted to *Revenge*" . *KBS Global*. July 7, 2005</ref><ref></ref> She then challenged herself by playing a cute yet ambitious tomboy who dreams of becoming a pilot in her first movie *Blue Swallow*.<ref>"Sky's the Limit for Han Ji-min" . *The Korea Times*. January 8, 2006.</ref>

A car accident on the set of *Wolf* seriously injured Han and her co-star Eric Mun,<ref></ref><ref>"Stars of TV drama *Wolf* injured in car accident" . *The Korea Herald*. Hancinema. January 24, 2006.</ref> causing the drama to be delayed repeatedly<ref>"늑대 (Wolf) Return Set For Summer". *Twitch Film*. February 14, 2006.</ref> until the decision was finally made to discontinue it with only three episodes aired. After a long recovery for Mun, the two actors reunited instead in the drama *Super Rookie Ranger* (also known as *Invincible Parachute Agent* or *Korea Secret Agency*).<ref>"Super Rookie Ranger" . *SBS Global*. Retrieved November 4, 2012.</ref>

Han continued to act in TV dramas, with memorable turns as a straitlaced resistance fighter in *Capital Scandal* and a royal concubine in the historical drama *Yi San*.<ref>"Interview with Han Ji-min (October 18, 2007), who plays the role of Song-yeon in Lee San, Wind of the Palace" . *MBC Global Media*. March 3, 2008.</ref> Known for her sweet, innocent and fragile image and her depictions of good girls,<ref></ref> Han surprised audiences with her performance as a sexy femme fatale in *Detective K: Secret of the Virtuous Widow*.<ref>"Han Ji Min: 'I Played Sexy and Mysterious Character'" . *KBS Global*. January 21, 2011.</ref>

### 2011–2017: Diverse genres
Han played a cynical veterinarian in *Padam Padam*, written by famous drama writer Noh Hee-kyung, which aired on newly launched cable channel jTBC.<ref></ref><ref>"Han Ji Min Likes Her Co-star Jung Woo Sung". *enewsWorld*. November 30, 2011.</ref> She then starred in *Rooftop Prince*, about a Joseon-era prince who time-travels to the 21st century where he meets his dead beloved's doppelgänger.<ref>"Han Ji-min to star in Park Yuchun drama". *10Asia*. January 18, 2012.</ref><ref></ref> The popularity of *Rooftop Prince* garnered Han several awards,<ref name="mnet" /><ref name="seoul"></ref> and increased her international profile, notably in Japan.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

In October 2013, Han left her talent agency S.M. Entertainment's SM C&C and joined Lee Byung-hun's BH Entertainment.<ref></ref>

In 2014, she starred in the opposites-attract romantic comedy film *The Plan Man*, about a methodical, mysophobic librarian who falls for a spontaneous, adventurous musician.<ref></ref><ref></ref> Following that, she played the dowager Queen Jeongsun in period film *The Fatal Encounter*, who is the fierce political rival of her step-grandson King Jeongjo.<ref></ref> Han next reunited with *The Fatal Encounter* co-star Hyun Bin in the 2015 suspense series *Hyde Jekyll, Me*, in the role of a circus master who falls for a theme park owner with split personality disorder.<ref></ref>

In 2016, Han starred in action thriller *The Age of Shadows*, based on the 1920s about anti-Japanese independence workers' plots and betrayals.<ref></ref> In 2017, Han was cast in romantic short film titled *Two Rays of Light* alongside Park Hyung-sik.<ref></ref>

### 2018–present: Critical acclaim
In 2018, Han starred in the film *Miss Baek*, playing the titular protagonist; an ex-convict who is emotionally closed up due to the social stigma.<ref></ref> Han's performance in the film won her the Best Actress award at the prestigious Blue Dragon Film Awards and Baeksang Arts Awards as well as other major award ceremonies.<ref name="blue" /><ref name="baeksang"></ref> The same year, she returned to the small screen with romance fantasy drama *Familiar Wife*, taking on the role of an ordinary wife with anger management issues.<ref></ref>

In 2019, Han starred in two television series; slice of life melodrama *The Light in Your Eyes*<ref name="dazzling"></ref> and romance melodrama *One Spring Night*.<ref name="spring"></ref><ref></ref> She then appeared in Kwak Jae-yong's romantic comedy film *A Year-End Medley* in 2021<ref></ref> and tvN television series *Our Blues* in 2022.<ref></ref>

In 2023, she starred in JTBC's *Behind Your Touch*, playing a veterinarian who gains psychometric powers. On September 24, 2024, she was appointed 'Blue Dragon Goddess', succeeding Kim Hye-soo. She was also the master of ceremonies for the 45th Blue Dragon Film Awards, which was held on November 29, 2024.<ref></ref>

Han starred in the drama *Love Scout* as Kang Ji-yoon, CEO of a successful headhunting company,<ref></ref> which  premiered on January 3, 2025.<ref></ref> She also starred in JTBC's *Heavenly Ever After* as Som-yi.<ref></ref> The following year, she starred in another JTBC series *The Practical Guide to Love* as Lee Ui-yeong, a hotel team manager.<ref name="ilgan"></ref>

## Personal life
### Relationship
On August 8, 2024, it was reported that after participating in KBS2's music talk show *The Seasons: Choi Jung Hoon's Night Park* in August 2023, Han and singer-songwriter Choi Jung-hoon stayed in contact and developed a romantic relationship. Both of their agencies confirmed that the two are dating.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref>

### Philanthropy and activism
A Social Welfare major from Seoul Women's University, Han is widely known for actively participating in volunteer work and fund drives, mostly through the relief organization Join Together Society. In 2009, she published her own book titled *We're Already Friends: Han Ji-min's Philippines Donation Book*, which includes stories and drawings of her time spent in a remote mountain village in the Philippines helping at a village school.<ref></ref> She then donated all the royalties to help in funding the education of children in North Korea and developing countries in Asia.<ref>"Actress Han Ji-min's Movie Picks" . *10Asia*. February 7, 2011.</ref> In 2012, she was designated as goodwill ambassador of the United Nations Environment Programme.<ref></ref>

On March 8, 2022, Han donated  to help Ukrainian victims in Russian invasion through the Korea Committee for UNICEF.<ref></ref> On May 6, 2022, Han donated  to the UN's international relief organization JTS (Join Together Society).<ref></ref> On June 3, 2022, Han became a member of the 'UNICEF Honors Club'.<ref></ref> On August 11, 2022, Han donated  to the Handicapped Home to assist in the 2022 South Korea floods through the Seoul Community Chest of Korea (Seoul Fruit of Korea).<ref></ref> On December 24, 2022, Han donated  to help support heating costs for people at risk of disaster through the National Disaster Relief Association Hope Bridge.<ref></ref> On February 9, 2023, Han donated  to help 2023 Turkey–Syria earthquakes through UNICEF of Korea.<ref></ref>
