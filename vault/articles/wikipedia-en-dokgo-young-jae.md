**Dokgo Young-jae** (born Jeon Young-jae on December 13, 1953) is a South Korean actor. He made his film debut in 1973, and also became active in the Dongrang Theatre Ensemble. Dokgo won Best Supporting Actor for *White Badge* at the Blue Dragon Film Awards in 1992, then became a household name late in his career with the television drama *My Mother's Sea* in 1993.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref>

His father, actor Dokgo Seong dominated Korean cinema in the 1950s and 1960s.<ref></ref>
