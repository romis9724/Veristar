**Yong Jun-hyung** (; born **Yong Jae-soon**, December 19, 1989<ref name="Russell2014"></ref>), known mononymously as **Junhyung**, is a South Korean singer-songwriter, record producer and actor. Junhyung rose to fame as a member of South Korean boy band Highlight (formerly known as Beast), formed in 2009. He departed from Highlight in 2019, amidst a controversy where he admitted to having watched a video of singer Jung Joon-young making out with a woman, which was filmed with consent but shared without.<ref name=":8"></ref>

As a solo artist, he released one extended play, *Flower* (2013), and one full-length album, *Goodbye 20's* (2018).<ref></ref><ref></ref> He also starred in the Korean dramas *Monstar* (2013) and *Coffee, Do Me a Favor* (2018)*.*<ref name=":1"></ref><ref name=":2"></ref>

## Early life and education
Junhyung was born in Seoul, South Korea. He changed his birth name Yong Jae-soon () to his current name when he was in 6th grade.<ref> |date=2010-12-03 |access-date=2011-06-01|language=ko}}</ref>

##  Career 

###  2007: Pre-debut 
In 2007, he joined the boy band Xing, which included Kevin Woo (ex-U-KISS). Junhyung was known by the stage name Poppin' Dragon while in Xing.<ref></ref>

###  2009–2016: Debut with Beast (now known as Highlight) 

Junhyung debuted under Cube Entertainment as a member of the boy band Beast in 2009. The group released their first extended play *Beast Is the B2ST* on October 14, 2009.<ref></ref> The group released a total of eight extended plays and three studio albums.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

###  2010–2018: Solo activities and discography with Highlight 
In 2010, Junhyung was featured as a rapper in songs by other artists, including Hyuna's debut single "Change,"<ref name="newsen1"></ref> the Asian remix of Amerie's "Heard 'em All,"<ref></ref> and G.NA's single "I'll Back Off So You Can Live Better."<ref name="G.Na MV"></ref> In 2011, Junhyung was featured on the songs "Heartsore Story" by Wheesung,<ref></ref> "A Bitter Day" by Hyuna,<ref></ref> "Be Quiet" by Kim Wan-sun,<ref></ref> and "Don't Act Countrified" by ALi.<ref></ref>

Junhyung released his first solo single, "Living Without You," on February 3, 2012.<ref></ref> The song reached #8 on the Gaon Digital Chart.<ref></ref> That summer, he made a cameo appearance on the sitcom *Salamander Guru and The Shadows.<ref name=":3" />* Later that year, Junhyung produced fellow Beast member Yang Yo-seob's debut solo EP, *The First Collage*, and was featured on the lead single, "Caffeine."<ref></ref>

In February 2013, Junhyung released the single "You Got Some Nerve" with LE of EXID and Feeldog of Big Star.<ref></ref> The song reached #13 on the Gaon Digital Chart.<ref></ref> Starting in May, Junhyung starred in the Korean drama *Monstar*, alongside Ha Yeon-soo and Kang Ha-neul.<ref></ref> The series aired on Mnet and wrapped up in August.<ref></ref> Junhyung won Best New Actor at the 2013 Korean Drama Awards for his depiction of a teen idol in the series.<ref>|language=ko|access-date=2019-03-15 |title=MTN News }}</ref>

Junhyung released his debut solo EP, *Flower,* on December 13, 2013.<ref></ref> The EP debuted at #4 on the Gaon Album Chart,<ref></ref> and the title track "Flower" peaked at #23 on the Gaon Digital Chart.<ref></ref>

In 2015, Junhyung starred in the Cube Entertainment online reality series, *Yong Jun-hyung's Good Life,* alongside his songwriting and music production partner Kim Tae-joo.<ref name=":4"></ref> From 2016 to 2017, he co-hosted the third season of the variety show *Hitmaker* alongside comedian Jeong Hyeong-don.<ref name=":5"></ref><ref></ref> In 2017, Junhyung was also a cast member on the first season of the variety show *It's Dangerous Beyond the Blankets.*<ref name=":6"></ref> He returned to the show for the second season in 2018.<ref></ref>

In 2016, Beast left Cube Entertainment, and formed their own label, Around Us Entertainment. The group debuted under the new name of Highlight.<ref></ref> As Highlight, they released two extended plays.*<ref></ref>*

Junhyung released his first solo full-length album, *Goodbye 20's*, on May 9, 2018.<ref></ref> The album debuted at #2 on the Gaon Album Chart.<ref></ref> That year, he also starred in the Korean drama, *Coffee, Do Me a Favor*, which is based on the webtoon of the same name.<ref name=":2" />

###  2019–2021: Career hiatus 
On March 14, Junhyung announced on Instagram that he was leaving Highlight, following new revelations regarding the KakaoTalk chatrooms scandal, saying he had watched a video sent to him by Jung Joon-young, which was later revealed being a video of Jung and a woman making out in a bar which was filmed with consent but shared to him without, and in one other instance having got knowledge of Jung filming another woman without her consent.<ref name=":0"></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref>

On November 15, 2021, Junhyung left Around Us Entertainment after his contract expired.<ref></ref>

###  2022–present: Solo activities 
On September 27, 2022, it was announced that Junhyung would be releasing a new album in the near future, which would be his first solo comeback in four years and also his first since his departure from Around Us Entertainment.<ref></ref> On October 11, he announced his first EP *Loner*, set to release on October 30, under his independent label Black Made.<ref></ref>

In October 2022 it was confirmed that Junhyung will hold his solo concert 'LONER's ROOM' at Yes24 Live Hall in Seoul on November 4 and 5, and will make a comeback with his new EP album "Loner" on October 30.<ref></ref> However, the label announced the postponement of the album due to the national mourning period following the Seoul Halloween crowd crush incident on the 29th.<ref></ref> And his solo concert was postponed as well.<ref></ref> Junhyung has confirmed the release schedule for the new album on November 10.<ref></ref>

On March 2, 2023, Junhyung will release "POST IT" (Feat. Xion) with the same name and "Til' the End", both composed by him.<ref></ref>

##  Controversy 
On March 11, 2019, the television program *SBS Eight O'Clock News* incorrectly reported that Junhyung was part of a group chatroom where musician Jung Joon-young shared hidden camera sex videos with acquaintances.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":7"></ref><ref></ref> On March 12, Junhyung clarified that, even though he had a chat with Jung, he was not part of the group chatroom.<ref name=":7" /><ref></ref> Seoul Metropolitan Police stated in April that they questioned him as a witness in the Jung Joon-young KakaoTalk chatrooms case.<ref> |language=ko |via=Naver}}</ref> In December, *KBS News* reported that the video Jung had sent to Junhyung depicted Jung consensually touching and filming a woman in a bar in Gangnam in November 2015, but the video was shared without her consent.<ref name=":8"/>

##  Philanthropy 
On March 7, 2022, Yong donated 10 million won to the Hope Bridge Disaster Relief Association to help the victims of the massive wildfire that started in Uljin, Gyeongbuk and has spread to Samcheok, Gangwon.<ref></ref>

##  Personal life 

###  Military service 
He quietly enlisted for his mandatory military service on April 2, 2019 as an active duty soldier.<ref></ref> On September 25, 2020, it was announced that Junhyung had suffered a knee injury, and he would complete the rest of his service as a public service worker.<ref></ref> He was discharged on February 26, 2021.<ref></ref>

###  Relationship 
On July 8, 2024, Hyuna and Yong's agencies jointly released an official statement saying that the two will be getting married in October. Their wedding ceremony will be held in private with their close friends.<ref></ref>
