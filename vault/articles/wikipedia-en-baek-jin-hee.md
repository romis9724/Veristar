**Baek Jin-hee** (; born February 8, 1990) is a South Korean actress.<ref name=":6"></ref> She began to be known for her role in *High Kick: Revenge of the Short Legged* (2011–2012) and rose to prominence for her role in *Empress Ki* (2013). She also starred in the television drama *The Real Has Come!* (2023).

## Career
Baek Jin-hee began her entertainment career after getting scouted on the streets by a talent agent. She has since then starred in the indie film *Bandhobi* (2009), where she plays a rebellious girl who befriends a Bangladeshi migrant worker;<ref name=":0"></ref><ref name=":1"></ref><ref name=":2"></ref> and sex comedy *Foxy Festival* (2010) where she plays a teenager with a thriving business selling her used underwear.<ref name=foxy></ref>

Baek then starred in the sitcom *High Kick: Revenge of the Short Legged* (2011–2012), playing a fresh graduate looking for employment. The series was popular and led to increased recognition for the actress.<ref name=kick></ref> This was followed with supporting roles in fusion historical drama *Jeon Woo-chi*<ref name=jwc></ref> and romance drama *Pots of Gold*.<ref name=gold></ref>

Baek had her breakthrough role as the villainous empress Tanashiri (Danashiri) in the period drama *Empress Ki* (2013–2014),<ref name=":3"></ref> She next starred as an orphaned casino dealer in melodrama *Triangle* (2014),<ref name=":4"></ref> and a passionate prosecutor in legal drama *Pride and Prejudice* (2014).<ref name=":5"></ref><ref></ref>

In 2015, Baek played the title role in family drama *My Daughter, Geum Sa-wol*, which was a hit and ended with national viewership of 33.6 percent.<ref name=daughter></ref>

In 2017, Baek played leading roles in the disaster drama *Missing 9*,<ref name=nine></ref> and office comedy series *Jugglers*.<ref name=jugglers></ref><ref></ref>

In 2018, Baek was cast as the female protagonist in the third installment of the *Let's Eat* franchise.<ref name=letseat></ref> The same year, she starred in the webtoon-based romantic comedy drama *Feel Good to Die*.<ref name=die></ref>

## Personal life
Baek Jin-Hee is the eldest of three sisters.<ref name=":6" />

On March 26, 2017, Baek's agency confirmed that Baek has been in a relationship with her *My Daughter, Geum Sa-wol* co-star, Yoon Hyun-min since April 2016.<ref></ref> On September 4, 2023, the couple announced their break-up after 7 years of dating.<ref></ref>
