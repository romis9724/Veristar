**Park Yong-kyu** (; born December 16, 1988), better known by the stage name **Park Seo-joon** (), is a South Korean actor. He first gained wide recognition for starring in the television series *Fight for My Way* (2017) and *What's Wrong with Secretary Kim* (2018), gaining further prominence with dramas *Itaewon Class* (2020) and *Gyeongseong Creature* (2023–2024).<ref></ref> He also starred in films *The Chronicles of Evil* (2015) and *Midnight Runners* (2017), and made his Hollywood debut with superhero film *The Marvels* in 2023.

## Early years and education
During his middle school years, Park Seo-joon happened to join an animation club by chance. Through this club, he had the opportunity to participate in an annual performance stage. He gained a lot of experience from being the center of attention, which led him to pursue his studies in the Acting Department at Seoul Institute of the Arts.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" />

While pursuing his studies in acting, Park encountered challenges and became overwhelmed by the constant presence of others. In 2008, he chose to enlist in the military, seeing it as an opportunity to gather his thoughts and organize his life. As such, Park believed that fulfilling his military service obligation should be his first priority. He was assigned to the Security Guard Battalion and completed his active military service at the Cheongju Correctional Facility.<ref name=":0"></ref><ref name=":1"></ref>

## Career
### 2011–2015: Beginnings and breakthrough
'' stage greeting in Busan, 2015]]

Park made his entertainment debut in 2011 by appearing in the music video of Bang Yong-guk's single "I Remember."<ref></ref> He played supporting roles in television dramas *Dream High 2* (2012),<ref></ref><ref name="dream"></ref> *Pots of Gold* (2013),<ref name="int" /> and *One Warm Word* (2013),<ref name="warm"></ref> and had his first leading role in *A Witch's Love* (2014).<ref name="int"></ref> From October 2013 to April 2015, he hosted *Music Bank*.<ref name="mb"></ref><ref></ref>

His breakout roles came in 2015 with the dramas *Kill Me, Heal Me* and *She Was Pretty*, where he starred alongside Hwang Jung-eum.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref name="pretty"></ref><ref></ref> The same year, Park featured in the thriller film *The Chronicles of Evil*.<ref name="evil"></ref>

### 2016–present: Mainstream popularity
In 2016, Park starred in the youth historical drama *Hwarang: The Poet Warrior Youth* alongside Go Ara and Park Hyung-sik, playing a young man of low birth, who becomes a legendary Hwarang warrior in the Silla period.<ref name="hwarang"></ref><ref></ref>

Park found success in the 2017 KBS2 romance comedy drama, *Fight for My Way*, where he played Ko Dong-man, a former taekwondo player with a painful past, who struggles to find his success as a mixed martial arts fighter, alongside Kim Ji-won.<ref name="fight"></ref> The television series was a major hit in South Korea and topped ratings in its time slot.<ref></ref>
The same year, he played his first big-screen leading role in the action-comedy *Midnight Runners* with Kang Ha-neul.<ref name="midnight"></ref><ref></ref> Park won the Best New Actor award at major film award ceremonies such as the Grand Bell Awards and the Korean Association of Film Critics Awards.<ref name="grand" /><ref name="critics" /> In 2018, Park starred in the romantic comedy drama *What's Wrong with Secretary Kim*, playing a narcissistic vice-chairman of a major corporation who falls in love with his secretary, played by Park Min-young.<ref></ref><ref name="kim"></ref> The series was a hit and Park received positive reviews from critics for his performance, being dubbed the "master of romantic comedy" by the Korean press.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref>

In 2019, Park starred in the action horror film *The Divine Fury* in the role of a martial arts champion with divine powers.<ref name="lion"></ref><ref></ref> The same year, Park served as the special juror for the Asiana International Short Film Festival.<ref></ref> He also made a guest appearance in the Oscar-winning movie *Parasite* (2019).<ref></ref> In 2020, Park starred in JTBC drama *Itaewon Class*, based on the webtoon of the same name. He played an owner of a bar-restaurant who successfully expands it into a franchise.<ref name="itaewon"></ref><ref></ref> The drama was a success, and was praised for its interesting story development and colorful performances. For his role, Park was nominated for Best Actor - Television at 56th Baeksang Arts Awards<ref></ref> In 2023, he starred in the sports film *Dream* as a soccer player turned coach named Yoon Hong-Dae, directed by Lee Byeong-heon,<ref name="dream1"></ref> and disaster-thriller film *Concrete Utopia* directed by Um Tae-hwa.<ref name="Utopia" /> He made his Hollywood debut, appearing in the MCU film *The Marvels* as Prince Yan of Aladna.<ref name="abscbnnews"></ref><ref></ref>

Park starred in the romance drama series *Surely Tomorrow* opposite Won Ji-an which premiered in JTBC in 2025.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

## Philanthropy
On February 10, 2023, Park donated  to the Hope Bridge National Disaster Relief Association to help victims of the 2023 Turkey–Syria earthquake.<ref></ref>
