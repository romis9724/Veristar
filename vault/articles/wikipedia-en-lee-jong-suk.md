**Lee Jong-suk** (, born 14 September 1989) is a South Korean actor and model. He debuted in 2005 as a runway model and gained recognition as an actor with television series *School 2013* (2012) and *I Can Hear Your Voice* (2013). He received further prominence with dramas *Doctor Stranger* (2014), *Pinocchio* (2014), *W* (2016), *While You Were Sleeping* (2017), and *Big Mouth* (2022).

## Education
Lee majored in Professional Motion Pictures and Art at Konkuk University and graduated from the university in 2016.<ref></ref>

## Career
### 2005–2010: Beginnings
At 15, Lee began his modelling career at the Seoul Collection walkway in 2005, making him the youngest male model to debut in the Seoul Collection program at Seoul Fashion Week. Since then, he has walked in a number of fashion shows.<ref></ref><ref></ref> Lee trained as an idol group member for three months, and had signed on with an agency to debut. However, he quit after the agency broke their promise of debuting him as an actor.<ref></ref> He took part in a selection for actors at SBS TV station when he was in middle school.<ref></ref> In 2010, Lee made his official acting debut in the South Korean TV series *Prosecutor Princess*. He also made his big-screen debut in the horror movie *Ghost*.

### 2011–2013: Rising popularity and breakthrough
Lee started to gain recognition following his supporting role in the hit drama *Secret Garden* where he played a talented young composer with a surly attitude and a forbidden crush on the second male lead.<ref name="secret"></ref><ref></ref>  
In 2011, he appeared in MBC's sitcom *High Kick Season 3* where he gained further popularity.<ref></ref><ref name="kick"></ref> Lee also co-starred in the film *R2B: Return to Base* in 2012, a loose remake of the critically acclaimed 1986 film *Top Gun*.<ref name="return"></ref>

Lee's breakout role came as a high school student in the teen drama *School 2013*.<ref name="school"></ref><ref></ref> He received his first acting award for the role at the 2012 KBS Drama Awards under the category Best New Actor.<ref name="kbs"></ref> He also ranked fifth according to a survey entitled 'Actors Who Lit Up 2013' by Gallup Korea.<ref></ref>

Following the success of *School 2013*, Lee starred in the critically acclaimed drama series *I Can Hear Your Voice* with Lee Bo-young. He played Park Soo-ha, an intelligent young man who can read minds.<ref name="voice"></ref> Originally slated for 16 episodes, the drama was extended by two episodes due to its successful ratings.<ref></ref> He received the Excellence Award in the male category at the Korea Drama Awards for his performance.<ref name="kda"></ref> Lee then starred in the sports film *No Breathing* and had a supporting role in the box office hit *The Face Reader*.<ref name="breathing"></ref><ref name="face"></ref>

### 2014–2018: Mainstream popularity
In 2014, he starred in teen romantic comedy *Hot Young Bloods*, playing a cheesy womanizer.<ref></ref><ref name="hot"></ref> Lee then starred in hit medical drama *Doctor Stranger*, playing a North Korean defector who works as a doctor in South Korea.<ref name="doctor"></ref> The drama was a success in China, gaining 400 million views.<ref></ref>
Lee next starred in *Pinocchio* opposite actress Park Shin-hye,<ref></ref> and played the protagonist Choi Dal-po, a first year reporter at a broadcasting company who struggles with the idea of justice and truth in a world where everyone wants to hide the facts.<ref name="pinocchio"></ref> *Pinocchio* was another hit, with a reported revenue of 6.2 billion won (US$5.62 million) for broadcasting rights in just one year.<ref></ref><ref></ref>
Lee's performances in the dramas *Doctor Stranger* and *Pinocchio* earned him acting accolades, including the Male Top Excellence Award at the 8th Korea Drama Awards.<ref name="korea"></ref> 
He also became the youngest actor to win the Best Actor award at the 27th Grimae Awards, chosen by television cinematographers.<ref name="grimae"></ref>

Lee then embarked on an Asia fan-meeting tour, and released a photobook titled *2014–2015 Asia Tour Story ~With~*, comprising photos taken of Lee's meetings with fans in seven Asian cities during the nine-month tour.<ref></ref> Lee was also cast in his first Chinese drama entitled *Jade Lovers* alongside actress Zheng Shuang. The drama, filmed in Shanghai, is described as a fantasy romance series set in the 1930s.<ref name="jade"></ref> Due to Lee's success in China, a wax figure of him went on display in Hong Kong's Madame Tussauds, which the actor unveiled himself in a ceremony.<ref></ref> 
In December 2015, Lee left his previous agency Wellmade Yedang and signed an exclusive contract with YG Entertainment on 10 May 2016.<ref></ref>

In July 2016, Lee returned to the Korean small screen with MBC's fantasy thriller *W* alongside Han Hyo-joo.<ref name="ww"></ref><ref></ref> The drama topped popularity charts in Korea<ref></ref> and Lee won the *Daesang*), which translates to "Grand Prize", is the highest honor given out at South Korean award ceremonies.}} (Grand Prize) at the year-end MBC Drama Awards.<ref name="daesang"></ref> The same year, he co-starred in the promotional web drama *7 First Kisses* for Lotte Duty Free.<ref name="kiss"></ref> In 2017, Lee starred in the crime film *VIP*, marking his first lead role as a villain.<ref name="vip"></ref><ref></ref> Lee then starred in the supernatural-procedural drama *While You Were Sleeping* alongside Bae Suzy in September.<ref name="sleep"></ref><ref></ref> In July of the same year, Lee was appointed Goodwill Ambassador for Korean tourism by the Korea Tourism Organization. The organization also released promotional videos featuring the actor, titled "8 Episodes of Korea", which focused on tourist spots in 10 different regions including Seoul, Gyeonggi, Gangwon, Jeju and Gyeongju.<ref></ref>

In 2018, Lee was cast in the three-episode drama *The Hymn of Death*, a remake of the 1991 film *Death Song*.<ref></ref><ref name="hymn"></ref> Lee signed with new management agency YNK Entertainment once his contract with YG Entertainment ended on 31 March 2018,<ref name="aman"></ref> but terminated his contract five months later.<ref></ref> and established his own agency "A-man Project".<ref name="aman" /> In 2019, he starred in his first romantic comedy drama *Romance Is a Bonus Book* alongside Lee Na-young.<ref name="supply"></ref><ref></ref>

### 2019–present: Military enlistment and comeback
In 2021, after finishing his military duty, he made a comeback with a cameo in *The Witch : Part 2. The Other One*.<ref></ref> Lee later confirmed for the official film *Decibel* alongside Kim Rae-won, Lee Sang-hee, and Cha Eun-woo. This will be the second time Lee will appear as the villain in a movie. With *VIP* being the first movie he is the villain.<ref></ref> Later in September 2021, Lee confirmed the MBC drama *Big Mouth* with Im Yoon-ah, coming back three years after his discharge from the military. It aired in 2022.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

## Personal life
Lee is best friends with fellow model-actor and *School 2013* co-star Kim Woo-bin, whom he has known since their modeling days.<ref></ref><ref></ref> He was the owner of the dining cafe, 89Mansion.<ref></ref> The cafe closed permanently on 14 September 2020 due to effects of the COVID-19 pandemic.<ref></ref>  Lee is confirmed to be in a relationship with singer-actress IU.<ref></ref>

### Military enlistment
Lee began his mandatory military service on 8 March 2019. Lee was deemed unfit to enlist as an active duty soldier as a result of suffering a car accident when he was 16 and tearing his anterior cruciate ligament. Therefore, he was confirmed to work as a public service officer, and was expected to be discharged on 31 December 2020.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref> He was discharged on 2 January 2021.<ref></ref>

### Philanthropy
On 7 March 2022, Lee donated 100 million won to the Hope Bridge Disaster Relief Association to help the victims of the massive wildfire that started in Uljin, Gyeongbuk and  spread to Samcheok, Gangwon.<ref></ref>
