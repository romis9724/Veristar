**Yoon Eun-hye** (; born October 3, 1984) is a South Korean singer and actress. She debuted as a member of South Korean girl group Baby Vox, staying with the group from 1999 to 2005, as well as WSG Wannabe, a female project group in 2022. Yoon has since moved on to acting and is best known for starring in the television dramas *Princess Hours* (2006), *The Vineyard Man* (2006), *Coffee Prince* (2007), *My Fair Lady* (2009), *Lie to Me* (2011), and *Missing You* (2012).

## Career

### 1999–2005: Debut with Baby V.O.X
Yoon debuted as a member of Baby V.O.X at the age of 15, replacing an ex-member (Lee Gai) in 1999. Yoon was the sub-vocal in the group.<ref></ref>

After she joined, the group released their third album *Come Come Come* in 1999. This album became their first major success, with their singles "Get Up" and "Killer" reaching the number one spot on the Korean music charts. In the same year, Yoon became a target of anti-fans. She was shot in the eye by an anti-fan who tried to make her blind with a mixture of soy sauce and vinegar using a water gun. She was rushed to the hospital where the doctors confirmed that her cornea was damaged.<ref></ref>

Yoon was also a frequent guest on SBS' popular game show *X-Man* from 2004 to 2005, gaining popularity for her "girl-warrior" image on the show and her love-line with Kim Jong-kook.<ref name=ten></ref><ref></ref>

Following fellow member Shim Eun-jin, Yoon ended her six-year activities as a member of Baby V.O.X in July 2005 when her contract with DR Entertainment expired.<ref></ref>

### 2006–2007: Breakthrough success with *Princess Hours* and *Coffee Prince*
In 2006, Yoon made her acting debut in romantic comedy *Princess Hours*, where she played an ordinary girl who becomes the crown princess due to an arranged marriage to the crown prince. Initially, fans of the *Goong* manhwa questioned her acting ability, and sent out petitions against Yoon as the lead role, requesting her to be replaced.<ref></ref> Despite the controversy, *Princess Hours* became a huge success across Asia and catapulted Yoon to Korean Wave stardom.<ref></ref><ref name=hallyu />

Yoon then made her debut on the big screen, starring in the film *The Legend of Seven Cutter* as a boxer.<ref></ref> She returned to television with a leading role in *The Vineyard Man* which aired on KBS in July 2006.<ref></ref> *The Vineyard Man* initially suffered low ratings, but received positive reviews for its heart-warming storyline and the cast's performances, and eventually rose in viewership.<ref></ref> Yoon won the Best Actress award at the 2006 Grimae Awards, chosen by cinematographers in every broadcasting station in Korea.<ref></ref>

In 2007, Yoon took on the leading role in MBC's drama *Coffee Prince*. She played a tomboy who was mistaken for a boy by her employer.<ref></ref> The drama was another huge success for Yoon, who was hailed by critics as an actress who brings a colorful shade to her character<ref></ref>, earning her the title of "Nation's Little Sister".<ref></ref><ref></ref> At the 44th Baeksang Arts Awards, Yoon beat out veteran actresses Kim Hee-ae and Park Jin-hee to win the coveted Best Actress award, becoming the youngest actress ever to win the award.<ref></ref> Riding the big success of *Coffee Prince* and *Goong*, Yoon became one of the highest-paid actresses in the industry.<ref name=hallyu></ref>

### 2008–2011: Career setbacks

In 2009, Yoon starred in KBS' romantic comedy *My Fair Lady*, playing an arrogant, strong-headed heiress of a rich business family.<ref></ref> Although *My Fair Lady* was a moderate success, Yoon was harshly criticized for her unnatural speech and accent as well as her regression in acting skills.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

Yoon returned to the big screen after five years with the coming-of-age film *My Black Mini Dress* (2010), based on the same titled chick lit novel by Kim Min-seo.<ref></ref> She then starred in SBS's romantic comedy series *Lie to Me* (2011), playing a smart government official who is unlucky in the love department.<ref></ref> However, both projects failed to achieve commercial success.<ref></ref>

### 2012–2015: Directorial debut, acclaim and *Goddess Fashion*
In 2012, Yoon made her directorial debut with the short film *The Knitting*, which was her first assignment as a graduate student at Chung-Ang University. The film was screened at the 17th Busan International Film Festival and competed in the Korean Short Film Competition.<ref></ref><ref></ref> It was also screened at the 38th Seoul Independent Film Festival as one of the five short films in the "New Choice" category.<ref></ref> The same year, she served as jury for the  'Face of Short Film Awards' at the Asian International Short Film Festival (AISFF).<ref></ref>

In late 2012, Yoon starred in MBC's melo-thriller *Missing You*, playing a victim of sexual abuse.<ref></ref><ref></ref> The role marked a turning point for Yoon, who received critical acclaim for her performance.<ref></ref><ref></ref> This was followed up with a leading role in KBS's romantic comedy *Marry Him If You Dare*, where she played a 32-year-old call center agent who travels back in time to change her future.<ref></ref><ref></ref> In 2014, Yoon was cast alongside Park Si-hoo in the Chinese-South Korean romance film *After Love*.<ref></ref>

In 2015, Yoon made a special appearance in the film  *Chronicle of a Blood Merchant*, directed by Ha Jung-woo.<ref></ref> She then joined the second season of *Goddess Fashion*, a Chinese fashion design survival program where the participants have to show off their own created designs to the judges. On August 29, 2015, Yoon was awarded No. 1 in an evaluation.<ref></ref>

### 2017–present: Return from hiatus
In 2017, Yoon made her return to the Korean entertainment industry by appearing as a regular in tvN's variety show *Dear Pet, We Need to Talk*.<ref></ref>

In 2018, Yoon was cast in the romantic comedy drama *Love Alert*. This marks her return to the small screen five years since her last drama *Marry Him If You Dare* in 2013.<ref></ref>

In 2019, Yoon starred in the 2-episode romance drama *Go Go Song*.<ref></ref>

## Controversies

### Conflicts with management
In July 2007, conflicts with Yoon's Management Eight Peaks surfaced; eventually Yoon filed a Certification Of Contents (COC) to cancel her contractual agreement with the company.<ref></ref> The contents of the first COC stated that in January 2007, Yoon was confirmed to star in the MBC drama *Que Sera Sera* and had even completed the script reading and rehearsal for the upcoming shoot. However, the company forced Yoon to withdraw for Yoon to star in a company-produced drama, and as an effect, she was replaced by Jung Yu-mi. After her withdrawal, Yoon took a two-day vacation to Gangwon Province in March. Eight Peaks claimed that they had no knowledge of this so-called incident and spread a rumour of Yoon's irresponsibility online which damaged her reputation.<ref name="이데일리 SPN: 함께 만들고 함께 즐기는 엔터테인먼트 포털"></ref> Yoon filed a second COC in August, which included evidence to support the disagreeable actions made by Eight Peaks. These actions included the improper handling and distribution/division of earnings and proceeds as well as the unilateral (one-sided) manner of making and carrying out decisions which caused immense problems."<ref name="이데일리 SPN: 함께 만들고 함께 즐기는 엔터테인먼트 포털"/>

Yoon and Eight Peaks finally came to a mutual agreement through careful negotiations and settled their disputes amicably. With her contract dispute resolved, it was reported in October 2008 that Yoon signed on with Kraze Entertainment as her new management company.<ref></ref>

### Plagiarism accusations
Following Yoon's win on the Chinese fashion program *Goddess Fashion*, in which she presented a white coat with collars and a unique fringe on the sleeves to give a feeling of wings following the film theme of *Chronicles of Narnia*, Yoon was accused of plagiarism by Korean fashion designer Yoon Choon-ho. The designer accused the actress of ripping off his design after being made aware of her piece on the show, and posted a comparison of his creation versus hers, stating that he and his team had worked hard on the piece from the F/W (Fall/Winter) line. Yoon Choon-ho additionally revealed that he had heard her and her stylist had picked up a sponsorship outfit just days ago.<ref></ref> Additionally, she was embroiled in another allegation of plagiarism when another design of hers was accused of being similar to a dress from 2015 F/W Dolce & Gabbana.<ref></ref>

She denied the allegations, saying that her idea for the outfit came from a 2008 Victor & Rolf style and the 2014 Lanvin Collection, which used feather decorations. She further condemned Yoon Choon-ho for trying to cash in on her fame.<ref></ref><ref></ref>
