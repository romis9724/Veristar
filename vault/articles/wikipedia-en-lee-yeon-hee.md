**Lee Yeon-hee** (; born January 9, 1988) is a South Korean actress. She is most known for her work in the television series *East of Eden* (2008), *Phantom* (2012),  *Miss Korea* (2013), *The Package* (2017); and in the films *A Millionaire's First Love* (2006), *M* (2007), and *Detective K: Secret of the Lost Island* (2015).

## Early life
Lee was born on January 9, 1988, in Haenam County, South Jeolla Province, South Korea.<ref></ref><ref name="Yeon-hee"></ref> In 2002, Lee successfully signed a contract with SM after she won the category of "Best Feature" in the SM Entertainment Best Youth contest.<ref name="youth"></ref> For the contest, she acted out a monologue, singing to Dana "Until the End of the World" and also did freestyle modeling poses. After she joined the company, she began intense training lessons on acting, singing, and dancing.

## Career
### 2002–2006: Beginnings
Soon after she joined SM, Lee began her career by starring in music videos for SM artists, such as boy band TVXQ, Moon Hee-jun, Kangta, Shinhwa, and Fly to the Sky, overtaking SM artists Kim Bomi (of M.I.L.K.) and Lee Jiyeon (commonly known as CSJH The Grace's Lina) as "The SM music video girl".<ref></ref>

Though Lee had relatively steady work between modelling in magazines, commercial ads and appearing in music videos, she did not officially debut until November 2004 where she starred as the younger version of the main character in the hit historical drama *Emperor of the Sea*, and appeared in the latter episodes of the daily drama *My Lovely Family.*<ref></ref><ref></ref> Lee had her first major role in television in MBC's mini-series *One Fine Day* (2006).<ref name="fine"></ref>

### 2007–2012: Rising popularity & acting criticism
]]
In 2006, Lee starred in romance melodrama *A Millionaire's First Love* alongside Hyun Bin.<ref name="million"></ref> She also made her singing debut for the film, singing "Do-Re-Mi" from *The Sound of Music* and *Insa* for the film's original soundtrack. Lee's innocent image in the film earned her the title of "Nation's First Love" in Korea.<ref></ref><ref></ref> Lee followed this with moderately successful film, *M* alongside Kang Dong-won; and starred alongside So Ji-sub in the short film, *U-Turn*.<ref name="mm"></ref><ref name="turn"></ref>

In 2008, she starred as one of the main cast in 47th MBC Anniversary Project *East of Eden*.<ref name="eden"></ref> Despite the drama's success, her acting in the series received overwhelming negative criticism about her vocalization, unexpressive facial expressions, and unnatural emotional expression as problems in portraying her character.<ref></ref> Lee returned to film, starring in *Hello, Schoolgirl* opposite Yoo Ji-tae. The story is based on the manhwa *Soonjeong Manhwa* by Kang Pool.<ref name="school"></ref>

In 2011, Lee starred opposite Jang Dong-gun, Joe Odagiri and Fan Bing-bing in Kang Je-gyu's big budget historical film *My Way*,<ref name="way"></ref> and in romance comedy series *Paradise Ranch* by S.M. Entertainment alongside label-mate Shim Chang-min of boy band TVXQ.<ref></ref><ref></ref> In 2012, Lee reunited with *U-Turn* co-star So Ji-sub in the crime thriller *Phantom*.<ref name="phantom"></ref><ref></ref>  Both Lee and So were named promotional ambassadors for cyber crime prevention by the National Police Agency in 2012.<ref></ref>

### 2013–present: Career resurgence and continued success
]]
In 2013, Lee made a special appearance in the historical drama *Gu Family Book*, where she received compliments for her improvement in acting.<ref name="gu"></ref><ref></ref>  She next starred in romantic comedy film *Marriage Blue*, which tells the story of an engaged couple who experience last-minute jitters and cold feet just a week before their wedding ceremony.<ref name="blue"></ref> Lee closed the year with television series *Miss Korea* to positive reviews, with many considering her role as her best performance to date.<ref name="miss-korea"></ref>

Lee further challenged herself with a provocative role as the mysterious and seductive Japanese geisha Hisako in the historical comedy *Detective K: Secret of the Lost Island* in 2015.<ref name="dk"></ref><ref></ref> She then starred in MBC's 54th anniversary drama, *Splendid Politics*.<ref name="splendid-politics"></ref>

In 2017, Lee starred in SBS's fantasy romance drama *Reunited Worlds* alongside Yeo Jin-goo.<ref name="reunited"></ref> She then starred in JYP Entertainment's travel romance drama *The Package* opposite Jung Yong-hwa,<ref name="package"></ref> and was complimented for her performance as a tour guide.<ref></ref>

In 2020, Lee starred in mystery drama *The Game: Towards Zero*, reuniting with Ok Taec-yeon with whom she starred in the 2013 film *Marriage Blue*.<ref></ref><ref></ref> However she was criticized for her bad acting skills by viewers.<ref></ref>

In 2020, Lee signed a contract with Vast Entertainment after her contract with SM expired.<ref></ref>

In January 2024, Lee signed with new agency Saram Entertainment after her contract with Vast Entertainment expired.<ref></ref>

## Personal life
On June 2, 2020, Lee married her non-celebrity boyfriend in a private ceremony.<ref></ref> On June 7, 2024, Lee announced that she was pregnant after four years of marriage.<ref></ref> Lee gave birth to her first child, a daughter, on September 11.<ref></ref>
