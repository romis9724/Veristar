**Jung Eun-ran** (; born 5 February 1986), better known by the stage name **Min Hyo-rin**,<ref></ref><ref></ref> is a South Korean actress, model and singer. She made her breakthrough with her role in the South Korean film *Sunny* (2011), followed by *The Grand Heist* (2012) – both cited as one of South Korea's highest-grossing box office hits.

## Career
Born in Dong-gu, Daegu, South Korea<ref name="MHR"/> as Jung Eun-ran, she adopted the stage name Min Hyo-rin when she began modeling for the clothing brand Flapper in 2006.<ref></ref> She then appeared in several music videos for Park Ki-young and F.T. Island. Before her singing debut, large teaser posters of her were displayed around the hot spots of Seoul as a promotional stunt. She released the album *RinZ* in 2007, and the single *Touch Me* in 2008.

In 2009 Min made her acting debut in the television series *Triple* opposite Lee Jung-jae and Song Joong-ki.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref> She was the lead actress in a 2010 web series adapted from Alain de Botton's novel *Romantic Movement*, with four different directors each shooting short films in various locations around Seoul.<ref></ref>

Min was paired with singer Seven in the reality show *Fox's Butler*,<ref></ref> and hosted the sixth season of cable channel Mnet's *Trend Report Feel* from 2010 to 2011.

In 2011, she and Choi Daniel starred in *Age of Milk*, a short film that was shot using a Samsung Galaxy S smartphone and aired on cable channel OCN.<ref></ref><ref></ref> Later that year, she was cast in the TV series *Romance Town* with Sung Yu-ri and Kim Min-jun,<ref></ref> and appeared in Song Jieun's music video "Going Crazy."

After being part of the ensemble cast of 2011 retro drama *Sunny* along with Shim Eun-kyung and Kang So-ra,<ref></ref> Min played Cha Tae-hyun's love interest in the 2012 period comedy *The Grand Heist*.<ref></ref><ref></ref> Both were huge box office hits. In 2012, Min co-starred with Jo Sung-ha and Park Jin-young in *A Millionaire on the Run* (also known as *Five Million Dollar Man*).<ref></ref><ref></ref> She next starred in *Twenty*, a film that depicts the lives and loves of three male friends in their twenties.<ref></ref>

In 2015, Min headlined the music drama *Persevere Goo Hae Ra*, produced by Mnet.<ref></ref>

In 2016, Min became a regular cast member in the KBS reality television show *Sister's Slam Dunk*.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

In March 2017, Min signed with Plum Entertainment after her contract with JYP Entertainment ended.<ref></ref> The same year, she starred in the two-episode drama *The Happy Loner* and the cycling film *Uhm Bok-dong*.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref>

## Personal life
Min began a relationship with singer Taeyang, member of the South Korean boy band Big Bang in 2013.<ref></ref><ref></ref> In December 2017, it was confirmed by their respective agencies that they were engaged.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref> They married on 3 February 2018 in a private church ceremony.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

On 27 September 2021, Min's agency announced that Min was pregnant and awaiting childbirth.<ref></ref> In November 2021, Min gave birth to a healthy son.<ref></ref><ref></ref>
