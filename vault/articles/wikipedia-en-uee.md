**Kim Yu-jin** (; born April 9, 1988),<ref name="After School profile"></ref> known professionally as **Uee** (; sometimes romanized as **U-**), is a South Korean singer and actress. She is best known for being a former member of South Korean girl group After School from 2009 to 2017, and has acted in various television dramas including *Queen Seondeok* (2009), *Ojakgyo Family* (2011), *Jeon Woo-chi* (2012), *Golden Rainbow* (2013), *High Society* (2015), *Hogu's Love* (2015)*, Marriage Contract* (2016), and *My Only One* (2018). On May 31, 2017, Uee left After School and its agency, Pledis Entertainment.

## Early life
Kim Yu-jin was born April 9, 1988, in Daejeon, South Korea. Her father, , is a retired professional baseball player and former head coach for the South Korean teams, Nexen Heroes<ref></ref><ref name="UEE/FATHER"></ref> and SK Wyverns.<ref></ref> She has an older sister, Kim Yu-na.<ref></ref> Uee attended Guwol Girls' Middle School and Incheon Physical Education High School. While in high school, she was a swimmer and competed in the Korean National Sports Festival.<ref></ref> She later graduated from Sungkyunkwan University,<ref>School of Art</ref> with a degree in Performing Arts.

## Career
### 2007–2008: Five Girls and career beginnings
Uee originally desired to be an actress and went to many auditions, but she debuted as a singer first.<ref name="transition" /> In 2007, Uee was a member of the girl group Five Girls () under Good Entertainment, which also included Yubin, Jun Hyoseong, Yang Jiwon, and G.NA. The group starred in a reality show on MTV called *Diary of Five Girls*, but disbanded before their scheduled debut due to Good Entertainment's financial troubles.<ref></ref>

Before debuting, she was once a hot topic in South Korea. On May 30, 2008, while watching her father's baseball game in Mokdong Stadium, she was caught by the camera and became known as the "Mokdong Girl".<ref></ref> In August 2008, after appearing as Yubin's friend in the MBC show *Introducing the Star's Friend*, she again became popular because of her friendship with the Wonder Girls member and her resemblance to Moon Geun-young.<ref></ref> Uee later joined Pledis Entertainment.

### 2009–2016: After School and acting career

In April 2009, Uee joined the girl group After School with their single "Diva".<ref></ref><ref></ref> She became known for her "honey thighs" and proved popular with the general public, often topping online search rankings on various Korean websites.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

In July, she made her acting debut in MBC's historical drama, *Queen Seondeok*, which was well received and won various awards.<ref name="QUEENSEONDUK/AWARDS"></ref> She was subsequently cast in SBS's musical drama *You're Beautiful*, which started airing in October.<ref name="YOUREBEAUTIFUL"></ref> Later that month, she joined the project group, 4Tomorrow, which consists Han Seung-yeon, Hyuna, and Gain; and released the single "Dugeundugeun Tomorrow" () on October 6, 2009.<ref></ref> That same year, she joined the MBC reality show *We Got Married* and was paired with Park Jae-jung.<ref></ref> Uee ranked eighth on Forbes Korea's list of most hardworking idols in 2009–2010.<ref></ref>

Uee had lead roles in two television dramas in 2011. In *Birdie Buddy*, she played a country girl who strives to become a professional golfer.<ref></ref> The drama's director, Yun Sang-ho, praised Uee's "flawless acting", saying, "I believe actors from idol groups such as Uee and Luna improve more quickly because they have so much talent and passion".<ref></ref> In *Ojakgyo Family*, Uee played a university student with a difficult family history.<ref name="transition"></ref> She earned praise for her natural acting and received Best New Actress awards at the Paeksang Arts Awards and KBS Drama Awards.<ref></ref><ref name="woochi"></ref> Uee released her first solo single, "Sok Sok Sok" (), on June 21, 2011.<ref></ref> She also co-hosted the variety show *Night After Night*.<ref></ref>

In January 2012, Uee became a permanent host on the KBS music show, *Music Bank*, along with Lee Jang-woo. She continued hosting the show until April 2013.<ref></ref> From November 2012 to February 2013, she starred as princess Hong Mu-yeon in *Jeon Woo-chi*, a historical drama set during the Joseon period. When she was cast for the role, Uee expressed her desire to be seen as a serious actor, saying "I want to do away with the 'idol singer-turned-actor' in this piece and really step up my performance".<ref></ref> During the shoot, she was praised for her ability to immerse herself in the character and give a detailed performance.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

Uee was a permanent cast member of SBS' survival variety show *Barefooted Friends* in 2013.<ref name="survivalshows"></ref> During the show, she performed the song "Hero" at a special concert. The song was produced by Duble Sidekick and co-written by Uee, and subsequently released on the soundtrack album *My Story, My Song* on August 19.<ref></ref><ref></ref> She had a lead role in the drama *Golden Rainbow*,<ref></ref> and received an Excellence Award at the 2013 MBC Drama Awards for the role.<ref name="2013excellenceaward"></ref>

In mid-2014, Uee was a member of the survival variety show *Law of the Jungle*, appearing in the Indian Ocean episodes.<ref name="survivalshows" /><ref></ref> In April of that year, she told *Ilgan Sports* she had lost some of her passion for singing and dancing, and plans to further her acting career once she graduates from After School.<ref></ref>

In early 2015, Uee starred in tvN's romantic comedy *Hogu's Love*, playing a national swimming champion.<ref></ref> She was then cast in the SBS drama *High Society*, playing a rich heiress who hides her identity in order to find true love.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref> In an interview, Uee acknowledged that some viewers were disappointed with her performance, and she had "never been more criticized for poor acting".<ref></ref> In October 2015, Uee joined the variety show, *Fists of Shaolin Temple*, where the cast members received martial arts training.<ref></ref>

Uee's next drama, MBC's *Marriage Contract*, began airing on March 5, 2016. She played single mother with a terminal illness who enters into a contract marriage.<ref></ref><ref></ref> In November, Uee starred in the MBC drama *Night Light* where she plays a poor woman who turns her life around with an opportunity.<ref></ref>

### 2017–present: Focus on acting and other works
Uee's contract with Pledis Entertainment ended on May 31, 2017, therefore she graduated from After School. In June 2017, she signed with new management agency Yuleum Entertainment.<ref></ref> She then starred alongside Kim Jae-joong in KBS's fantasy romance-comedy drama *Manhole*.<ref></ref><ref></ref> In 2018, Uee starred in the weekend dramas *My Contracted Husband, Mr. Oh* together with Kim Kang-woo;<ref></ref><ref></ref> and *My Only One*.<ref></ref>

In July 2019, it was confirmed that Uee signed an exclusive contract with King Entertainment.<ref></ref> She was later picked as part of the cast for *Cabin Crew Season 2* in 2019. The show features four celebrities challenging to experience the real work of flight attendants, from job interview to training. Season 2 ended early 2020.<ref></ref>

On June 12, 2020, Uee appeared as guest on the Korean variety show *I Live Alone*. The show features single celebrities and their everyday lives, both in and out of their homes.<ref></ref><ref></ref> At the same month, she also appeared as a guest in the show *Dogs Are Incredible* along with her dog, Mango. On August 1, she also appeared on another variety show *Omniscient Interfering View* along with her manager.<ref></ref><ref></ref> The show observes the lives of celebrities and their managers. Uee also played as Han Ji-won in the 7th episode of *SF8* titled *Love Virtually* alongside Choi Si-won.<ref></ref> The episode was aired on September 25, 2020. After her love for spicy foods was shown her recent *I Live Alone* appearance, she was selected as the official endorser for Samyang Food's 'Fire Chicken Sauce' Series.<ref> 홍보관  삼양식품|url=http://127.0.0.1:8080/kor/publicity/press/view.do?seq=844|website=127.0.0.1|language=ko|access-date=December 29, 2020}}</ref>

In 2020, Uee hosted Glance TV's *Last Fit with U-ie*, a fitness program showcasing beginner to advanced exercises that can be done at home while under quarantine. The first broadcast started in August via Naver TV.<ref></ref> On October 23, Uee hosted the 2020 Live in DMZ Concert along with Super Junior's Leeteuk. The concert was held in Gyeonggi Province to commemorate the 2nd anniversary of the September 2018 Pyongyang Joint Declaration. The concert was broadcast on October 24 via MBC.<ref></ref>

On February 8, 2021, it was reported that Uee had cut ties with King Entertainment.<ref></ref> Following the report, the agency confirmed that their exclusive contract with the actress has expired.<ref></ref><ref></ref> On March 29, 2021, it was announced that she had signed an exclusive contract with Lucky Company.<ref></ref><ref></ref> On June 3, it was announced that Uee will star in the variety program called *Spicy Girls* along with Sunny, Kim Shin-young, and Choi Yoo-jung.<ref></ref> She later went on to star in the drama *Ghost Doctor* alongside Rain and Kim Bum.<ref></ref><ref></ref> The drama premiered in January 2022.

## Personal life
In 2016, it was confirmed by Uee's representatives that she was dating fellow actor Lee Sang-yoon.<ref></ref><ref></ref> The couple broke up after a year of dating.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

In July 2017, Uee confirmed that she was dating *Law of the Jungle* co-star Kangnam.<ref></ref><ref></ref> This confirmation came after Korean website *Dispatch* released photos of them on a date in *Apgujeong*.<ref></ref> Uee initially denied the rumors. After 3 months of dating, the couple announced that they had split up.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

Uee has been a target of hate and criticism since her debut, in particular with her weight and an alleged sponsorship she received from a large company.<ref></ref> In 2018, her agency Yuleum Entertainment announced that they will take legal measures against the malicious and defamatory comments towards their talent.<ref></ref>

During her *I Live Alone* appearance, she revealed that rumors surrounding her weight caused her a lot of stress, leading her to eat only one meal a day for eight years.<ref></ref><ref></ref> Since then she began to focus more on her health while getting back on track with her career.

### Philanthropy
On April 8, 2022, Uee donated  to international relief and development NGOs to fund hygiene products, such as sanity wear kits and sanitary pants coupons, alongside a monthly information brochure for 120 youths entering young women's homes across the country.<ref></ref>
