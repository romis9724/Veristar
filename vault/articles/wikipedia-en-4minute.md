**4Minute** (, often stylized  in all caps) was a South Korean girl group formed by Cube Entertainment,<ref name="yonhap" /> consisting of members Jihyun, Gayoon, Jiyoon, Hyuna and Sohyun.<ref name="KBS"></ref> The group was known for its edgy dance-pop style and "girl crush" image that attracted a large female fan base.<ref></ref><ref name=":0"></ref>

The group debuted in June 2009 with the single, "Hot Issue."<ref name=":1" /> They ultimately released one full-length Japanese album, *Diamond* (2010), one full-length Korean album, *4Minutes Left* (2011), seven Korean extended plays, and multiple singles.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref name=":0" />

The group received several major awards, including the Rookie Award at the 2009 Golden Disc Awards,<ref></ref> the K-pop New Artist Award at the 2010 Billboard Japan Music Awards,<ref name=":2" /> and the Bonsang Award at the 2011, 2012, and 2014 Seoul Music Awards. 4Minute disbanded in June 2016 after unsuccessful contract renewal negotiations between Cube Entertainment and the majority of the group members.<ref name="yonhap" />

##  History 
###  2009–2010: *For Muzik*, *Hit Your Heart* and *Diamond* 
 On May 14, Cube Entertainment announced the formation of a five-member girl group, called 4Minute, and revealed that its first member would be former Wonder Girls member Kim Hyuna.<ref></ref> Prior to the official announcement, the group was unofficially called the "Hyuna group".<ref name="hyuna group"> | date = May 14, 2009 | access-date = January 15, 2014 | last = Rhu (류) | first = Myung-June (명준) | language = ko | archive-date = January 16, 2014 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20140116114855/http://www.segye.com/content/html/2009/05/14/20090514000549.html | url-status = live }}</ref> The next member, Nam Ji-hyun, was revealed on May 22.<ref></ref> The remaining members were announced the following day.<ref></ref> 4Minute released its first single, "Hot Issue", on June 15, and had their first television performance on MNet Countdown on June 18.<ref name=":1"></ref> On June 21, the group's van was raided by fans, who stole clothing and other items. Cube Entertainment said, "It was very unfortunate that such an ordeal occurred."<ref></ref> On August 20, the group released its first mini-album called *For Muzik* along with its second single, "Muzik".<ref></ref> The song won an Inkigayo Mutizen award. On August 27, South Korean radio and television network KBS banned the playing of "Won't Give You" () for what they considered to be overly sexual lyrical content.<ref name="JapanTimes" /> The lyrics in question, in the style of a first-person narrative, express an unwillingness on the part of the subject to "give" herself or her heart to her partner. Cube Entertainment responded to the ban by stating that the song was intended to be "about the pure feelings of a girl to a guy" and that they were very disappointed by the decision.<ref></ref> On October 15, the single "What a Girl Wants" was released.<ref></ref> On December 2, 4Minute, with Korean singers Mario and Amen, released the Christmas song "Jingle Jingle".<ref></ref> The group recorded a remix with American artist Amerie for her song "Heard 'em All" which was part of the Asian version of her fourth album.<ref></ref> The song featured Korean rapper Yong Jun-hyung of label mate Beast.<ref></ref>

On January 5, 2010, it was announced that the Universal Music Group would be assisting 4Minute with an international launch of its album.<ref></ref> The group toured Taiwan, Philippines, Thailand, and Hong Kong.<ref></ref> On May 5, the group debuted "Muzik" as a Japanese single,<ref name="First"></ref> and on May 8, held its first concert in Japan.<ref name="JapanTimes"></ref><ref name="First" /> The single reached number 21 on the Oricon weekly singles chart.<ref></ref> On May 19, 4Minute released a mini-album entitled *Hit Your Heart*, and its title track "HuH".<ref></ref> The single featured fellow label mates Beast, who rapped during song "Who's Next?."<ref></ref> "HuH" peaked at number 3 on the Gaon Singles Chart.<ref name="gaon 2"></ref> On May 24, South Korean FM station "Voice of Freedom" included the "Huh" song in its propaganda broadcasts to North Korea.<ref></ref> On July 5, the group released the music video for "I My Me Mine", which was the second single from *Hit Your Heart*.<ref></ref> On July 19, the song "Superstar" was digitally released for the Korean TV show Superstar K2.<ref></ref> The group performed for Celebration at Marina, one of the line-up activities for the Singapore Youth Olympic Games 2010.<ref></ref> On July 30, the Japanese version of  "I My Me Mine" was released.<ref></ref> On October 12, the third Japanese single.<ref name="Third"></ref> In December, 4Minute held Japanese concerts *4Minute Energy Live Volume 2: Diamond* in Tokyo and Osaka. Its Japanese album *Diamond* was released on December 15 and debuted at the number 18 on the Oricon chart.<ref></ref>

###  2011–2012: *4Minutes Left*, *Volume Up* and "Love Tension" 

On January 13, 2011, the group performed at the DiGi Live K-Pop Party 2011 in Malaysia at Stadium Negara with Beast and G.NA.<ref></ref> On January 20, the group received a Bonsang Award at the 20th Seoul Music Awards.<ref></ref> In February 2011, the group received the Kpop New Artist Award for 2010 at the Billboard Japan Music Awards.<ref name=":2"></ref> On February 23, the group modeled for bridal designer Yumi Katsura at her 2011 Paris Grand Collection Tokyo Fashion Show held in Tokyo's Ryōgoku Kokugikan, where the group performed songs "Muzik" and "Why".<ref></ref> On March 12, the group performed at the 50th Anniversary Music Wave Concert in Thailand.<ref></ref> Its fourth Japanese single, "Why" was released as the theme song to the TV Asahi drama, *Akutō: Jūhanzai Sōsahan*.<ref></ref><!--Although the translation only states that the song was 'featured.' the original text says 오프닝 테마곡, or opening theme song)-->The music video was released in February, followed by a single release on March 9.<ref></ref> "Heart to Heart" was released as a music video on March 29, and starred CN Blue's Jungshin.<ref></ref> On March 29, the digital-only mini-album *Heart to Heart* was released.<ref></ref> On April 5, 4Minute released the Korean album, *4Minutes Left*.<ref></ref><ref name="Battery"></ref> The music video for "Mirror Mirror" was released on the same day.<ref></ref> The album includes "Mirror Mirror", and songs from *Heart to Heart* and *Diamond*.<ref name="Battery" /><ref name="heartcount" group="note">Because the *Heart to Heart* tracks were put on the release of the full album *4minutes Left* a week later, *Heart to Heart* is not counted as a mini-album, Cube Entertainment instead labeled *Volume Up* to be the group's third mini-album, and *Name is 4Minute* to be the fourth mini-album. Countdown* on April 14.<ref></ref> The song reached number two on the Gaon Chart.<ref></ref> 4Minute collaborated with Japanese singer Thelma Aoyama for her single, "Without U", which has both Japanese and English versions.<ref></ref> Hyuna was featured in SS501's Heo Young-saeng's title track from his first solo mini album, *Let It Go*.<ref></ref><ref></ref> On July 5, Hyuna released a solo mini album, *Bubble Pop!* and the music video for the title track, *Bubble Pop*.<ref></ref> Although the performances for *Bubble Pop* the song was concluded prematurely due to Korea Communications Commission review committee, the music video was a viral hit, gaining 10&nbsp;million views on YouTube as of August 2011.<ref></ref> On August 3, the music video *Freestyle* was released as an endorsement track for the video game company *JCE*.<ref></ref> On August 15, 4Minute posted the music video for the Japanese version "Heart to Heart".<ref></ref> On September 7, the group released "Heart to Heart" as its fifth Japanese single, which ranked 15 on Oricon Daily Singles chart.<ref name="H2H Japan"></ref> The group also released its first DVD, *Emerald of 4 Minute*, which ranked second on the Oricon Daily DVD Chart.<ref name="H2H Japan" /> In October, the TV Tokyo drama "Welcome to the El-Palacio" featured 4Minute's newest song, "Ready Go", as its opener.<ref></ref><ref></ref> On December 6, "Ready Go" was later released as the sixth Japanese single.<ref></ref>

On April 9, 2012, 4Minute released the mini-album *Volume Up* along with the title track "Volume Up" and its music video.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref> The single "Volume Up" reached number one on M! ###  2013–2014: *Name Is 4Minute*, Is It Poppin'? and *4Minute World* 
From January 26 to February 21, 4Minute participated in the 2013 United Cube Concert tour at venues in China, Korea and Japan.<ref></ref> On April 26, 4Minute released the fourth mini-album *Name Is 4Minute* and the single "What's Your Name?", which was produced by Brave Brothers.<ref></ref> Promotion for the single and album involved a set of teaser images where the members were decorated in fantasy-inspired sets and neon tones.<ref></ref> In the fourth week of May, the song reached number one on the Gaon Single Chart, making it the group's first chart topper for Gaon.<ref></ref> On the edition of June 1, the single reached number one on the *Billboard* K-pop Hot 100.<ref></ref> On June 28, 4Minute released the summer single "Is It Poppin'?", also written and produced by Brave Brothers. In an interview with Newsen, a Cube Entertainment spokesperson said that the song was "neither a sexy or charismatic concept, but a cute concept – a hip hop dance song that goes well with the summer feel."<ref></ref> Its Korean title "물 좋아?" (romanized: Mul Joha?, lit. "How's the Water?"), is a slang phrase that men use to ask if the girls in a club are hot.<ref name="popdust20130626"></ref> KBS announced that the lyrics for "Is It Poppin?" were unqualified for broadcast because they contained the English word "ass". 4Minute planned to remove the word from the video for re-evaluation.<ref></ref> *Billboard* K-Town columnist Jeff Benjamin tweeted whether the Brave Brothers' new thing was to sample old-school-sounding R&B.<ref></ref> Benjamin also noted that similarly to "What's Your Name," "Brave Brothers uses a similar formula for 4minute's new track, but it all feels smoother." He explains the track "uses subdued snares, a twinkling Ellie Goulding "Lights"-esque sample and a soft, recurring vocal sample. Despite sounding busy on paper, the song flows nicely together as a sweet little blend of R&B and electro-pop."<ref></ref> The song peaked at number 9 on the Gaon Digital Chart<ref name="goandigital-poppin"></ref> and number 6 on the *Billboard* K-Pop Hot 100,<ref name="billboard-poppin"></ref> selling 606,713 digital downloads.<ref name="poppin-sales"></ref> In September, 4Minute finished its contract with Universal Music Japan, and shut down the group's Japanese official website as well as its fanclub site, 4Nia Japan.<ref></ref>

On January 20, 2014, Brave Brothers released the single "Only Gained Weight" which featured 4Minute members Hyuna, Gayoon and Sohyun; the song reached number five on the Melon chart<ref></ref> and Gaon.<ref></ref><ref></ref> The group's fifth mini-album, *4Minute World*, along with its featured track "Whatcha Doin' Today?", was released on March 17.<ref></ref> The group promoted the song on various Korean music shows along with the track "Wait A Minute". The shows included KBS's Music Bank, MBC's Show! Music Core, SBS's *Inkigayo* and Mnet's *M! Countdown* from March 20–23. The single became the group's first single to debut at number 1 in Korea. On March 30, 4Minute won against 2NE1 and Girls' Generation on *Inkigayo*. And on April 3, they also won on *M! Countdown* which took place at Japan.  On June 7, 4Minute performed "Whatcha Doin' Today" on Dream Concert along with other Kpop idols including their labelmates Beast, Apink and BTOB. On October 17, during a performance at a local festival in Seongnam, south of Seoul in South Korea, at least 16 people were killed and 11 injured in an accident where a ventilation grate that people were standing on collapsed.<ref></ref> On October 28, 4Minute was chosen as one of the 10 winners of "Style Icon Awards" for their trendy, stylish, and influential fashion, and also performed at the awards show.<ref></ref>

###  2015–2016: *Crazy*, *Act. 7* and disbandment 
On January 26, 2015, Cube Entertainment revealed that 4Minute would pre-release a track from their next mini-album titled "Cold Rain", marking the first time the group has released a ballad as a single.<ref></ref> On February 9, 4Minute released their sixth EP *Crazy*, along with the title track's music video.<ref></ref><ref></ref> Their EP debuted at number 1 on *Billboard*'s World Albums Chart.<ref></ref> On April 4, 4Minute held a solo concert *4Minute Fan Bash in Myanmar*, with around 7,000 fans in attendance.<ref></ref>

On January 20, 2016, it was announced that the group would release their seventh mini album *Act. 7* on February 1.<ref></ref> 4Minute collaborated with American producer Skrillex on the lead single, "Hate".<ref></ref> On June 13, it was announced that the group would disband following their contract expiration, with only Hyuna renewing her contract with the management agency.<ref name="yonhap"></ref> A Cube Entertainment representative stated, "As the contract end date approaches, the members have been in discussions for renewal, but while the agency tried to persuade half of them to continue the group, it ended up accepting the decision by the four members that they will not continue as a group." The representative added, "Due to the different opinion of the members, for all intents and purposes, the group will disband."<ref></ref>
