**Choi Min-ho** (; born December 9, 1991),<ref name="Russell2014"></ref> known mononymously as **Minho**, is a South Korean singer, songwriter, rapper and actor. In May 2008, he debuted as a member of South Korean boy band Shinee which later became one of the best-selling Korean artists. Aside from group activities, he debuted as an actor in November 2010 in KBS2's drama special *Pianist*.<ref name="Minho debut acting"/> He has since landed roles in television series such as *Salamander Guru and The Shadows* (2012), *To the Beautiful You* (2012), *Medical Top Team* (2013), *My First Time* (2015), and *Hwarang: The Poet Warrior Youth* (2016). He made his feature film debut in 2016 with *Canola*. As a soloist, he has released the digital singles "I'm Home" (2019) and "Heartbreak" (2021). He released his debut solo EP *Chase* in 2022.

## Career
### 2008–2009: Career beginnings and Shinee

Choi was discovered at the SM Casting System in 2006<ref></ref> and modeled for Ha Sang-beg's Seoul Collection F/W 08–09 in March 2008.<ref>민호맞아? 파격 변신 샤이니 티저 사진 공개  Edaily via *Nate*. Retrieved December 15, 2010.</ref> One month later, Choi was chosen as a member of the group Shinee. The five-member boy group debuted on May 25, 2008, on SBS' *Inkigayo*.<ref></ref> The group's first EP, *Replay*, was released on May 22<ref name="shineetimes"></ref> and debuted on the Korean music charts MIAK at number ten,<ref></ref> peaking at number eight.<ref></ref> 

Choi began participating in the rap making of Shinee's songs with their first studio album *The Shinee World*, under the guidance of rap instructor JQ.  JQ praised Choi's writing ability and pointed out that he has many good ideas and "a few genius moments here and there". He was especially pleased with Choi's rap lyrics for Shinee's 2009 single "Juliette".<ref name="JQ"></ref>

In 2009 and 2010, Choi appeared in both the Korean and Japanese versions of Girls' Generation's music video "Gee".<ref name="gee"></ref> He was also featured in the music video of VNT's debut song "Sound" in 2010.<ref name="sound"></ref> In 2009, Choi joined the popular show *Let's Go! Dream Team* as a regular cast member.<ref name="dreamteam"></ref>

### 2010–2015: Acting debut

In 2010, Choi joined the variety show *Oh! My School* as a cast member<ref name="ohmyschool"></ref> and began hosting the music program *Show! Music Core* alongside bandmate Onew, Miss A member Suzy and T-ara member Jiyeon.<ref name="mucore1"></ref> He made his acting debut in KBS2's drama special, entitled *Pianist*, with actress Han Ji-hye.<ref name="Minho debut acting">SHINee Minho to make acting debut through KBS drama . *10Asia* November 9, 2010. Retrieved November 13, 2010</ref> In 2011, Choi was cast in SBS' sitcom, *Salamander Guru and The Shadows*. He played the role of a genius hacker.<ref name="salamander"></ref>

In 2012, Choi was confirmed to take on the lead role, alongside f(x)'s Sulli and Lee Hyun-woo, in *To the Beautiful You*, a Korean adaptation of the manga *Hana-Kimi*.<ref name="ttby"></ref><ref></ref>
The drama started broadcasting on August 15, 2012, on SBS.<ref></ref> In preparation for his role as a high jump gold medalist, Choi received personal training from coach Kim Tae-young, former national high jump athlete and a member of the Korea Association of Athletics Federations, for a month and a half.<ref> '아그대' 민호, 높이뛰기 선수로 완벽 '빙의' . Naver. (July 18, 2012). Retrieved on October 14, 2012.</ref> In December 2012, Choi joined dance project group SM The Performance alongside labelmates from TVXQ, Super Junior, Shinee, and Exo.<ref></ref>

Choi began hosting the variety show *Mamma Mia* in April 2013, but departed after a month due to Shinee's touring schedule.<ref name="mammamia"></ref> He resumed his role as an MC on *Show! Music Core* after a two-year absence, this time hosting alongside Kim So-hyun and Noh Hong-chul.<ref name="mucore2"></ref> In July 2013, Choi returned to the small screen with MBC's *Medical Top Team*.<ref name="medical"></ref> His performance received a positive response and was praised for his growth as an actor.<ref></ref> In August, he participated in the reality series *Star Diving Show Splash*,<ref name="splash"></ref> but it was cancelled after four episodes after several contestants were injured.<ref></ref>

In 2015, Choi was cast as the lead in OnStyle's drama *My First Time* together with actress Park So-dam, which was broadcast in September. It was the first drama premiered by OnStyle, with a total of eight episodes.<ref></ref><ref name="myfirsttime"></ref>

### 2016–2020: Big screen debut, solo music and military enlistment
'' in November 2016]]
In early 2016, Choi extended his popularity as an actor. He was cast in several projects, including a historical Korean drama entitled *Hwarang: The Poet Warrior Youth*, which began airing on December 19, 2016, on KBS2.<ref name="hwarang"></ref> His debut movie project titled *Canola*, alongside Youn Yuh-jung and Kim Go-eun and directed by Yoon Hong-seung (pseudonym Chang), was filmed in 2015 and released on the big screen in May 2016.<ref name="canola"></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref> He was cast in a supporting role in a film entitled *The Princess and the Matchmaker*, led by Lee Seung-gi and Shim Eun-kyung. The movie is the second installment in a planned trilogy by Jupiter Films after the 2013 box-office hit *The Face Reader*.<ref name="princess"></ref><ref></ref> Choi was then cast in the movie *Derailed* alongside veteran actor Ma Dong-seok, which was directed by Lee Seong-tae and premiered at the 2016 Cannes Film Festival.<ref name="derailed"></ref><ref></ref> The movie had its world premiere screening at the 21st Busan International Film Festival in October 2016.<ref></ref>

In 2017, Choi took part in the JTBC web drama entitled *Somehow 18* alongside Lee Yu-bi.<ref name="somehow18"></ref><ref></ref> As a result of high online viewer ratings, the web drama was broadcast on television through JTBC.<ref></ref> After receiving a positive response for his acting performance in *Derailed*,<ref name="jinroh"/> Choi was cast in science fiction action thriller *Illang: The Wolf Brigade*, the Korean adaptation of the Japanese anime *Jin-Roh: The Wolf Brigade*.<ref name="illang"></ref> Choi stated that he chose the role because he wanted to work with director Kim Jee-woon.<ref></ref> The film released on July 25, 2018.<ref></ref><ref name="jinroh"></ref> In September 2017, Choi received the Special Award at the  due to his widespread popularity in the country as well as for his performances in various dramas. Choi was the first Korean artist to be presented an award at the show.<ref name="special award"/><ref></ref> On November 15, Choi was included in *Vogue*s list of the "Sexiest Men Alive".<ref></ref> Later that year, Choi was cast in a four episode mini drama *The Most Beautiful Goodbye*, which is a remake of the 1996 drama of the same name.<ref name="tmbg"></ref>

In March 2019, Choi released his debut solo single, "I'm Home", as part of SM Station.<ref name="Enlist"></ref> He then enlisted for the Marine Corps on April 15 as part of his mandatory military service,<ref name="Enlist" /><ref></ref> where he was recognized as an "exemplary marine soldier" for forgoing early discharge to participate in defense drills.<ref></ref> He completed his military service on November 15, 2020.<ref></ref>

### 2021–present: Further acting roles, *Chase* and *Call Back*

In January 2021, Choi resumed activities as part of Shinee.<ref></ref> He also ramped up participation in various shows including a special appearance in web series *Lovestruck in the City*<ref name=":1" /> and main role in reality show *Law of the Jungle – Pioneers*.<ref name=":2" /> In September 2021, Choi debuted as a radio host on Naver Now's broadcast of *Best Choice*, a sports talk-variety show where Choi, well known as an "athletic idol", delivered sports news and talked with fans and various guests about "his favorite things".<ref name="bestchoice"/> He made a special appearance in *Yumi's Cells* as Chae Woo-gi, the titular character's coworker and crush.<ref name="yumiscells"/> Choi released the digital single "Heartbreak" on December 21.<ref></ref>

He appeared in the horror film *New Normal*, directed by Jeong Beom-sik. It was selected as the closing film of the 26th Bucheon International Fantastic Film Festival.<ref name="newnormal"/> His first Japanese songs, "Romeo and Juliet" and "Falling Free", were digitally released on August 24.<ref></ref> He released his debut solo EP, *Chase*, on December 6, 2022.<ref></ref> Choi starred in the Netflix series *The Fabulous* alongside Chae Soo-bin later that month.<ref name="fabulous"/>  He held his first fan concert, 2024 Best Choi's Minho Fan-Con Multi-Chase, on January 6–7, 2024, at Jangchung Arena in Seoul.<ref></ref> He also released the digital single "Stay for a Night".<ref></ref> In August 2024, he starred in the JTBC drama *Romance in the House*.<ref name="rith"></ref> In September, he appeared in his first play, *Waiting for Waiting for Godot*, alongside Lee Soon-jae.<ref name="wfwfg"></ref> He portrayed the role of Val, an understudy continually waiting to go on stage; however, the production was cut short due to Lee's health issues.<ref name="wfwfg2"></ref>

Choi released his first studio album, *Call Back*, on November 4, 2024.<ref></ref> He held his first solo concert, Mean: Of My First, later that month, at the Hwajeong Tiger Dome in Seoul.<ref></ref> He later held shows in Taiwan, Japan, Macau and the Philippines.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref> He appeared in the play *Rendezvous* in April 2025.<ref name="rendezvous"></ref> In September, he resumed his role as Val in a new production of *Waiting for Waiting for Godot*.<ref name="wfwfg2"/> He participated in the sports reality show *Rising Eagles*, captaining a celebrity basketball team.<ref name="risingeagles"></ref> He released the single album *Tempo* on December 15.<ref name="tempo"></ref>

## Personal life

Choi graduated from Konkuk University's affiliated high school on February 10, 2010.<ref></ref>  After taking the college admissions test the previous year, he was admitted in 2010 to Konkuk's part-time admittance for the arts and culture film major and graduated in 2015.<ref>샤이니 민호, 건국대 정시 합격   *Today Korea*. Retrieved December 15, 2010.</ref> His father, Choi Yun-kyum, is a well-known South Korean soccer coach. Choi inherited his father's ability and showed his skills in shows like KBS2's *Our Town's Physical Variety* and won high praise from Lee Young-pyo, a retired member of the national team. Choi also led his team to victory at the Idol Futsal World Cup Competition.<ref></ref> Originally, Choi wanted to become a professional football player like his father but he disapproved of his decision stating: "Frankly, I didn't want him to follow my path, [...] Back when I was playing, it was difficult to make it in that world, very abusive back in those days. I would stay home for a couple of hours and then be off to training camp. It pained me to see my son asking me to stay over for the night." His father also added that from the football manager's point of view, he didn't think his son had it in him to make it in that field, stating Minho is a strong-willed person, but has a small frame.<ref name="koreadaily"></ref>

## Other ventures
### Endorsements
 event in 2015]]
Choi has endorsed numerous brands and has been listed among the highest paid advertising models in South Korea, reportedly receiving 300 million won as his annual advertising rate.<ref></ref> Food company Our Home selected Choi as its first solo advertising model due to his "healthy and bright image".<ref></ref> He appeared in advertising for SK Telecom, promoting its LTE network.<ref></ref> In 2015, Choi was selected as a model for luxury fashion brand Coach alongside Choi Soo-young and Victoria Song, as part of a global project targeting people in their twenties.<ref></ref> He attended the Coach 2016 S/S fashion show in New York.<ref></ref> In 2017, he became the face of outdoor brand K2, promoting its sportswear line,<ref></ref> and casual wear brand Andew.<ref></ref> He attended Givenchy's 2018 fashion show in Paris,<ref></ref> where he was listed among the best dressed celebrities.<ref></ref> The same year, Choi was chosen as the official face of the Korea Sale Festa, the largest shopping event in Korea, becoming the first-ever Korean idol to promote it.<ref></ref>

Pernod Ricard Korea announced in January 2022 that they selected Ju Ji-hoon and Choi as the new brand ambassadors of the modern classic scotch whisky brand Ballantine's and launched the new global campaign "Time Well Spent".<ref></ref> The following year, he was named the new face of Godiva, and participated in campaigns for Valentine's Day and White Day.<ref></ref> In 2025, he became the advertising model for the McDonald's McSpicy Shanghai Burger.<ref></ref>

###  Philanthropy and ambassadorships 
In 2011, Choi was named an Honorary Ambassador for Youth by South Korea's Ministry of Gender Equality and Family. He was chosen for the role because he embodied the policy goals of "healthy physical growth" and "cultivation of a sound mind".<ref></ref> He has participated in several campaigns for UNICEF. In 2014, Choi and Girls' Generation member Yoona were appointed as ambassadors for Uniheroes, a fundraising branch of UNICEF.<ref></ref> He later took part in UNICEF's "#Imagine" campaign, which was first introduced in November 2014 to commemorate the 25th anniversary of the United Nation's Convention on the Rights of the Child.<ref></ref>  In 2022, the Korea Creative Content Agency, together with the Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism, unveiled the second part of their immersive cultural project "Age of Light", in which Choi appeared as a virtual figure in the artificial intelligence experience booth, "Gwanghwa In".<ref></ref>  In March 2023, he donated 5 million won to Cheongju North Chungcheong Province for the love hometown project.<ref></ref>

Choi has supported multiple campaigns promoting sport. In November 2017, Choi, alongside First Lady of the United States Melania Trump, attended the "Girls Play 2" campaign at the US embassy in Korea, an event organised to promote the 2018 Winter Olympics.<ref></ref><ref></ref> In July 2021, the Korean Professional Football Federation appointed Choi as one of five K-League Ambassadors to help promote the league to the public.<ref></ref> He was selected as an "Olympic friend" by the International Olympic Committee to promote the 2024 Winter Youth Olympics in Gangwon. He participated in the Olympic torch relay in Chuncheon on December 5,<ref></ref> and later attended the 2024 Summer Olympics in Paris.<ref></ref> He was appointed as an ambassador for the Korean Sport & Olympic Committee, with the aim of promoting the 2026 Winter Olympics.<ref></ref>
