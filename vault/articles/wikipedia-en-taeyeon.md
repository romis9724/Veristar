**Kim Tae-yeon** (; born March 9, 1989), known mononymously as **Taeyeon**, is a South Korean singer. She debuted as a member of girl group Girls' Generation in August 2007, which went on to become one of the best-selling artists in South Korea and one of the most widely known K-pop groups worldwide. She has since participated in other SM Entertainment projects, including Girls' Generation-TTS, SM the Ballad, Girls' Generation-Oh!GG, and the supergroup Got the Beat.

In 2015, Taeyeon released her debut extended play (EP) *I*, whose titular single reached number one on South Korea's Gaon Digital Chart. In 2016, she released the number-one single "Rain" as part of the SM Station project, followed by her second EP *Why*, which peaked atop the Gaon Album Chart. Taeyeon's debut studio album, *My Voice*, was released in 2017 and yielded the top-five singles "11:11", "Fine" and "Make Me Love You". Taeyeon ventured into the Japanese music scene in 2018 with the digital single "Stay", followed by the 2019 EP *Voice*. Her second studio album, *Purpose* (2019), featured the number-one single "Four Seasons" and the top-5 single "Spark". Her third studio album *INVU* (2022) included the number-one titular single as well as top-10 singles "Weekend" and "Can't Control Myself".

On television, Taeyeon appeared on the reality shows *We Got Married* (2009) and *Begin Again* (2019) and hosted *Queendom 2* (2022). She is currently a cast member of *Amazing Saturday* (2020–present). Additionally, Taeyeon has recorded soundtracks for various television dramas and films, including "If" for *Hong Gil-dong* (2008), "Can You Hear Me" for *Beethoven Virus* (2008),<ref></ref> and the chart-topping "All About You" for *Hotel del Luna* (2019).

Having sold over one million physical albums and twenty million digital singles as of 2021, Taeyeon is one of the best-selling solo artists in South Korea. She has received various awards and nominations, including seven Golden Disc Awards, six Seoul Music Awards, five Circle Chart Music Awards, four Melon Music Awards, and four Mnet Asian Music Awards.

## Life and career
### 1989–2008: Early life and career beginnings

Taeyeon was born on March 9, 1989, in Wansan District,<ref name="Taeyeon"></ref><ref name="Kim Taeyeon"></ref> Jeonju, South Korea.<ref name="Russell2014 birthdate"></ref><ref name="birthplace"></ref> Her family consists of her parents, an older brother, and a younger sister Kim Hayeon, who made her solo debut in 2020.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref> Having believed that she was "only talented" at singing, Taeyeon first dreamed of becoming a singer at a young age.<ref></ref> During her second year in middle school, Taeyeon accompanied her father to SM Academy, a former music institution and auxiliary affiliation of SM Entertainment, to pursue vocal lessons. Although at the time her father did not encourage Taeyeon to become an entertainer, he was persuaded by the school's principal to give his daughter a chance after she recognized Taeyeon's "clean" voice. During the next year, Taeyeon accompanied her father from their hometown to Seoul every Sunday to receive vocal lessons from The One.<ref name="smacademy"></ref> She named BoA, Whitney Houston and Hikaru Utada as her pre-debut inspirations.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

Taeyeon trained under The One for three years. In a 2011 interview, he described Taeyeon as a "smart" student, someone "unlike others who came to him with dreams of becoming a celebrity... [she] wanted to be a singer and reach the top with her own talents." In 2004, he "wanted to acknowledge Taeyeon's potential" by giving her the chance to be in his song "You Bring Me Joy".<ref></ref><ref></ref> That same year, she won first place at the SM Youth Best Competition and signed a contract with SM Entertainment. The training process was so tough that she almost quit at one point; she persisted, however, and debuted as a member of girl group Girls' Generation in August 2007.<ref name="earlylife"></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref> Both the president of SM Academy and The One later named Taeyeon as one of their most memorable students.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref> Taeyeon graduated from Jeonju Art High School and was awarded the school's Lifetime Achievement Award in 2008.<ref></ref>

### 2008–2015: Original soundtracks, TTS and SM The Ballad

Taeyeon sang "7989" (featuring Kangta), a track from both Girls' Generation's eponymous debut album (2007) and Kangta's fourth studio album, *Eternity* (2008).<ref></ref> Taeyeon began to gain popularity through her various television soundtrack releases, recording "If" for the KBS2 drama *Hong Gil Dong* (2008) and "Can You Hear Me" for MBC's drama *Beethoven Virus* (2008).<ref name="talentedvocalist"></ref> "If" was later deemed more popular than its respective drama,<ref></ref> and "Can You Hear Me" won a Popularity Award at the 2008 Golden Disk Awards.<ref></ref> Both tracks remain among Taeyeon's most well-known hits.<ref name="talentedvocalist" /> During this period, she also recorded "It's Love", a duet with Girls' Generation member Sunny, for the MBC drama *Heading to the Ground* (2009).<ref></ref>

Following a radio hosting job in 2009–10,<ref></ref> Taeyeon journeyed into acting, making her theatrical debut in a lead role in the 2010 musical *A Song to the Sun*. She played the part of Kaoru Amane, a teenager who suffered from xeroderma pigmentosum, and learned to play a guitar for the role. Her performance in the sold-out shows received good press despite the musical's critical reviews of the musical itself.<ref name="midnightsun"></ref><ref name="The_Musical_report"></ref> Jang Gyeong-jin of *10Asia* praised Taeyeon's "distinct vocals" and commented that her acting stood out among idol stars who had ventured into musical theatre,<ref></ref> while Kim Ji-yeong from *The Dong-A Ilbo* felt "Taeyeon's optimistic and charming personality made her the perfect match" for the role.<ref></ref> Despite the favorable response, Taeyeon later described musicals as difficult, stating that her weak concentration made it hard for her to act and sing at the same time.<ref></ref>

The same year, Taeyeon worked with Korean songwriter-composer Ahn Young-min. She recorded "Like A Star", featuring The One, which reached number one on the Gaon Digital Chart.<ref name="likeastar"></ref><ref name="LASchart"></ref> Her next single, "I Love You", for the SBS drama *Athena: Goddess of War* soundtrack, reached number two.<ref name="ILUchart"></ref> Ahn recalled his impression of the latter song: "I was surprised at how well Taeyeon could sing. This is a rather mature song, so I would be lying if I said I didn't have my doubts, but she blew me away. I was moved to tears listening to her singing."<ref name="ahn2"></ref> In 2011, Taeyeon lent her voice to Kim Bum-soo's "Different", a single on his seventh album *Solista: Part 2*.<ref></ref> The song debuted at number two on the Gaon Digital Chart.<ref name="different-gaon"></ref> Taeyeon and Kim Bum-soo reunited at his concert two years later for a live cover version of the latter's 2008 song "Man and Woman".<ref></ref>

After their first collaboration on the drama *Beethoven Virus* (2008), musical director Lee Pil-ho was determined to collaborate with Taeyeon again on the soundtrack for MBC's melodrama *The King 2 Hearts* (2012), having her record the main theme song "Missing You Like Crazy".<ref></ref> It was released in March 2012 and debuted at number two on both the Gaon Digital Chart and K-pop Hot 100.<ref name="MULCgaon"></ref><ref name="MULCbillboard"></ref> Taeyeon's vocals received positive feedback from the song's composer Park Hae-woon: "She fully understood the song in its entirety and expressed each detail very precisely...even though this song requires the lowest note that a female singer can produce, Taeyeon executed them perfectly."<ref></ref> The song was named the most popular soundtrack at 2012 Seoul Drama Awards.<ref name="2012seoulaward"></ref> Later in the year, she contributed vocals to the song "Closer", for the SBS drama *To the Beautiful You*, which charted at number seven on both the Gaon Digital Chart and K-pop Hot 100.<ref name="Closergaon"></ref><ref name="Closerbillboard"></ref>

In April 2012, a subgroup of Girls' Generation called TTS was formed, its lineup consisting of Taeyeon, Tiffany and Seohyun. Their debut EP, *Twinkle*, was met with much success and became South Korea's eighth-highest selling album of the year.<ref name="triplecrown"></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref> The subgroup went on to release two more EPs: *Holler* (2014) and *Dear Santa* (2015).<ref></ref><ref></ref> In January 2013, Taeyeon and Tiffany sang a duet, "Lost in Love", which was featured on Girls' Generation's fourth Korean album, *I Got a Boy*.<ref></ref> Two months later, she recorded "And One", written and composed by Kangta, for the SBS drama *That Winter, the Wind Blows*.<ref></ref> It debuted at the top of *Billboard*'s K-pop Hot 100<ref name="andone-billboard"></ref> and reached number two on the Gaon Digital Chart.<ref name="AndOnegaon"></ref> In July 2013, Taeyeon was invited to record the theme song "Bye" for the soundtrack of the film *Mr. Go* after its musical director was left with a "deep impression" having heard her cover version of a song during a radio appearance.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

In February 2014, Taeyeon joined the roster of SM the Ballad, a ballad group initially formed by SM Entertainment in 2010, contributing vocals to their second album *Breath*.<ref name="breath" /> Her lead single of the same name, a duet with Shinee's Jonghyun, peaked at number three and six on South Korea's Gaon Digital Chart and K-pop Hot 100 Chart, respectively.<ref name="Breathgaon"></ref><ref name="Breathbillboard"></ref> She also recorded a solo song, "Set Me Free", for the album.<ref name="breath"></ref> Her other releases in 2014 included "Colorful" for JTBC's campaign "The World Is More Beautiful Because We're Different" and "Love, That One Word" for the SBS's drama *You're All Surrounded*; the latter entered Top 10 on both Gaon Digital Chart and K-Pop Hot 100.<ref></ref><ref name="LTOWgaon"></ref><ref name="LTOWbillboard"></ref> In February 2015, Taeyeon accepted a personal request from f(x)'s Amber to feature in "Shake that Brass", the lead single from her debut album, *Beautiful*.<ref></ref>

### 2015–2016: Solo debut and first solo concert

Taeyeon began working on her solo projects in late 2014, believing the "right time" had come after eight years with Girls' Generation.<ref></ref><ref></ref> She released her debut extended play (EP), *I*, in October 2015.<ref name="billboard-I"></ref> It peaked at number two on South Korea's Gaon Album Chart,<ref></ref> and charted atop the US *Billboard* World Albums.<ref name="billboard-I" /> As of 2019, it has sold 160,000 copies in South Korea.<ref name="gaontaeyeon"></ref> The commercial success of *I*, according to Yonhap News Agency, was the main contributor to SM Entertainment's leading music sales in the fourth quarter of 2015.<ref></ref> The title track, which features rapper Verbal Jint, incorporated elements of pop rock that marked a departure from the ballad sound she had been known for until then.<ref name="billboard-I" /> The song peaked at number one in South Korea,<ref></ref> where it has sold over 2.5 million digital units.<ref></ref> To accompany her solo release, Taeyeon held a series of small concerts called Taeyeon's Very Special Day,<ref></ref> and a reality show, OnStyle's *Daily Taeng9Cam*.<ref></ref> She was awarded Best Female Artist at the 2015 Mnet Asian Music Awards<ref></ref> and at the 2016 Golden Disk Awards.<ref></ref>

Taeyeon featured on Yim Jae-beom's "Scars Deeper than Love" (2015),<ref></ref> Verbal Jint's "If The World Was a Perfect Place" (2015),<ref></ref> and Crush's "Don't Forget" (2016).<ref></ref> "Don't Forget" sold over 2.5 million copies in South Korea.<ref></ref> In February 2016, she recorded "Rain" as the inaugural release of SM Entertainment's digital music platform SM Station.<ref name="koreaherald_2016-02-04"></ref> "Rain" peaked at number one in South Korea,<ref></ref> where it sold over 2.5 million digital copies.<ref></ref> Taeyeon and Super Junior's Kyuhyun were featured in a commercial for bottled water brand Jeju Samdasoo, for which she recorded a cover of "The Blue Night of Jeju Island".<ref></ref> She also became a spokesperson for Sword and Magic, a game developed by the company Longtu Korea, for which she recorded a rendition of BoA's 2003 song "Atlantis Princess".<ref name=":0"></ref>

Taeyeon's second EP, *Why* (2016), incorporated elements of more upbeat genres such as EDM and tropical house.<ref></ref><ref></ref> It peaked atop the Gaon Album Chart,<ref></ref> and charted at number two on the US *Billboard* World Albums.<ref></ref> The EP has sold 120,000 copies as of 2019.<ref name="gaontaeyeon" /> It spawned two singles: "Starlight" featuring Dean and the title track, which peaked at numbers five and seven, respectively, in South Korea.<ref></ref> The EP's associated concert, Butterfly Kiss, made Taeyeon the first Korean female artist to embark on a full-scale concert while still being a member of an active girl group.<ref name="butterflykiss"></ref> In September 2016, Taeyeon recorded "All With You" for SBS's television drama *Moon Lovers: Scarlet Heart Ryeo*.<ref></ref> Her next single, "11:11", was released in November 2016. It peaked at number two on the Gaon Digital Chart<ref></ref> and sold 1.4 million copies by 2017.<ref name="2016gaon">Cumulative sales for "11:11" in 2016 and 2017:

* </ref> *Billboard* ranked it the seventh best K-pop song of 2016.<ref></ref> *The Dong-a Ilbo* in December 2016 named Taeyeon as "Voice of the Year", "Singer of the Year", and "Most Hardworking".<ref></ref> At the 2016 Mnet Asian Music Awards, Taeyeon was awarded Best Female Artist.<ref></ref>

### 2017–2019: *My Voice*, Japanese debut and *Purpose*

Taeyeon's first studio album, *My Voice*, having been in the production process for over a year, was released in February 2017 and included the single "Fine".<ref></ref><ref name="billboard-myvoice"></ref> Both the album and song were well-received, topping both the Gaon Album Chart and Gaon Digital Chart, respectively.<ref></ref><ref></ref> On April 5, 2017, a deluxe edition of the album was released, accompanied by the single "Make Me Love You".<ref></ref> The song reached number four on the Gaon Digital Chart.<ref></ref> Taeyeon also featured in the single "Lonely" from labelmate Jonghyun's second compilation album, which was released April 24, 2017.<ref></ref> The following month, Taeyeon embarked on her first Asian tour, Persona.<ref name="persona"></ref><ref name="firstinhistory"></ref>

In December 2017, Taeyeon released her special EP *This Christmas: Winter Is Coming*.<ref></ref> The album debuted at number two on the Gaon Album Chart and at number six on the *Billboard* World Albums Chart.<ref></ref><ref></ref> Hong Dam-young of *The Korea Herald* highlighted Taeyeon's "calm and undeniable vocal prowess" in the album while praising the songs as "the most sophisticated Christmas tunes" of the year.<ref></ref> Its lead single, "This Christmas," debuted at number two on the Gaon Digital Chart.<ref></ref> To promote the album, Taeyeon held a two-day Christmas concert titled The Magic of Christmas Time.<ref></ref>

In June 2018, Taeyeon released her third EP, *Something New*.<ref></ref> It peaked at number three on the Gaon Album Chart<ref></ref> and at number four on the *Billboard* World Albums Chart.<ref></ref> That same month, Taeyeon embarked on a Japan Showcase Tour, available only to members of SM Entertainment's official Japanese fan club.<ref></ref> The tour visited Fukuoka, Nagoya, Tokyo and Osaka. Following the success of her sold-out showcase, Taeyeon released her debut Japanese single, "Stay", on June 30, 2018. The single was accompanied by a B-side titled "I'm The Greatest".

In August 2018, Taeyeon and MeloMance released a collaboration track titled "Page 0" for the third season of SM Station, marking Taeyeon's second time participating in the project.<ref></ref> That same month, Taeyeon was announced to be part of Girls' Generation's second subgroup, Oh!GG, which consisted of the five members that re-signed with SM Entertainment; the group released their first single, "Lil' Touch" in September.<ref name="OhGG"></ref> Taeyeon embarked on her second Asian tour from October 2018 to January 2019.<ref></ref> On October 12, it was announced that the tour would play a show at Hong Kong's AsiaWorld Expo Arena, making her the first South Korean female solo artist to hold a solo concert at the venue.<ref></ref> Encore concerts of her tour were announced in early 2019 and took place at Seoul's Jamsil Arena on March 23 and 24.<ref></ref>

Taeyeon released the digital single "Four Seasons" and its B-side "Blue" in March 2019.<ref></ref> "Four Seasons" charted at number six on the *Billboard* World Digital Songs<ref></ref> and topped South Korea's Gaon Digital Chart for two consecutive weeks.<ref>

Taeyeon released her second studio album, *Purpose*, on October 28, 2019.<ref></ref> The album peaked at number two in South Korea<ref></ref> and spawned the single "Spark", which reached number two on the Gaon Digital Chart.<ref></ref> The album was reissued on January 15, 2020, with three bonus tracks, including the single "Dear Me".<ref></ref> To promote her recent releases, Taeyeon embarked on her third Asian tour, which began in Seoul on January 17, 2020.<ref></ref> The shows—as well as her second concert tour in Japan, which was set to visit eight cities—were all eventually cancelled due to the COVID-19 pandemic.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

On March 9, 2020, Taeyeon was set to release the digital single "Happy" in celebration of her birthday; however, she postponed the single's release to May 4 following the death of her father.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref> The song reached number four on the Gaon Digital Chart and number nine on *Billboard*'s World Digital Songs Chart.<ref></ref><ref name=":1"></ref> In November 2020, Taeyeon released her second Japanese-language EP *#GirlsSpkOut*. The title track of the same name featured Japanese rapper Chanmina, marking Taeyeon's first Japanese collaboration.<ref></ref> In the same period, Taeyeon became a regular cast member on tvN's music variety show *Amazing Saturday - DoReMi Market*.<ref></ref> Taeyeon released her fourth Korean-language EP *What Do I Call You* on December 15, 2020.<ref></ref> The album peaked at number four on the Gaon Album Chart and sold over 120,000 units in South Korea, making Taeyeon the first female artist to have five different albums sell over 100,000 units each on the Gaon Album Chart.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

Taeyeon was featured in Taemin's song "If I Could Tell You", released as part of his EP *Advice* (2021).<ref></ref> Her next digital single, "Weekend" was released on July 6, 2021.<ref></ref><ref></ref> The single peaked at number four on both the Gaon Digital Chart and the *Billboard* K-pop Hot 100 Chart.<ref name="gaon"></ref><ref name="kpop-hot"></ref> The following month, she joined the travel variety show *Petkage* alongside label-mate Kim Hee-chul.<ref></ref> On August 30, 2021, Taeyeon was featured in Key's pre-release single "Hate That...".<ref></ref> On November 28, 2021, Taeyeon recorded "Little Garden" for tvN's television drama *Jirisan*.<ref></ref> On December 27, 2021, Taeyeon was revealed as a member of the supergroup Got the Beat alongside groupmate Hyoyeon. On January 17, 2022, Taeyeon released her new digital single "Can't Control Myself", which serves as a pre-release single for her third studio album, *INVU*, which was released on February 14.<ref></ref><ref></ref> The album peaked at number two on South Korea's Gaon Album Chart.<ref></ref> "INVU" charted at number eight on the *Billboard* World Digital Songs<ref></ref> and topped South Korea's Gaon Digital Chart for four consecutive weeks.<ref></ref> She was awarded Best Female Solo Artist at the 2022 Genie Music Awards.<ref></ref>

On May 5, 2023, it was announced that Taeyeon would hold her fifth concert, The Odd of Love, starting from June 3.<ref></ref><ref></ref>

On November 27, 2023, Taeyeon released her fifth extended play, *To. ## Artistry and voice
Taeyeon's musical style has evolved over the course over her career, especially since debuting as a solo artist. In her early years, she predominantly recorded ballads for television soundtracks.<ref name="firstinhistory" /> With the popularity of songs such as "If" (2008) and "Can You Hear Me" (2009), she was referred to as the "representative singer of popular OSTs".<ref name="talentedvocalist" /> Taeyeon opted for a pop rock sound in her 2015 debut single "I";<ref name="billboard-I" /> her 2016 single "Rain" incorporates elements of jazz and R&B;<ref name="koreaherald_2016-02-04" /> and "Why" blends EDM, R&B and tropical house.<ref name="goddess"></ref><ref name="star"></ref> Vibe's Yoon Min-soo described Taeyeon's musical style as an example of "K-Soul": "I can feel that sort of emotions from the ballads Taeyeon sings. If you can feel a deep soul from a singer, that's 'K-Soul'."<ref></ref>

Taeyeon has earned praise for her vocal talent,<ref name="talentedvocalist" /><ref></ref><ref></ref> which has received attention from several major South Korean producers. In 2009, on MBC's *Yoo Jae-suk & Kim Won-hee's Come to Play*, Park Jin-young of JYP Entertainment expressed his wish to work with Taeyeon because of her "ability to tell a story through singing."<ref></ref> Korean composer Yoo Young-seok noted Taeyeon's ability to sing as expressively as "a woman who has been divorced seven times."<ref name="musicinterview"></ref> Music producer Jo Young-soo, who wrote her song "Like a Star", was likewise impressed by Taeyeon's ability to convey a variety emotions through her vocal performance.<ref></ref> Taeyeon was selected as the best K-pop vocalist by 40 anonymous music industry officials in October 2015,<ref name="bestkpopvocalist"></ref> and was named the best female vocalist in their twenties by twelve Korean music companies in April 2016.<ref name="osen_best"></ref>

Taeyeon has received praise for her vocal register and singing technique. Shin Yeon-ah, leader of girl group Big Mama and a vocal instructor at Howon University's Department of Applied Music, called Taeyeon a vocalization master and appreciated her breath control and capability to maintain impeccable vocal conditions even while dancing, attributed to her "well-developed core muscle strength". Seo Geun-young, a faculty member in the Postmodern Music Department at Kyung Hee University, highlighted Taeyeon as a vocalist who excels in maintaining the intricate balance between the thyroarytenoid and cricothyroid muscles while showcasing clarity in both low and high vocal ranges. Oh Han-seung, head of the Vocal Department at Dong-ah Institute of Media and Arts, highlighted Taeyeon's distinctive presence, emphasizing her ability to make the songs stand out without relying on elaborate arrangements or strong beats. Oh described Taeyeon's sound as "trendy, elegant, and sometimes poignant, covering a frequency range suitable for prolonged listening". Consequently, Oh praised Taeyeon's vocal quality as an ideal example of "natural compression", devoid of fatiguing frequencies that tire the ears.<ref></ref>

## Public image and impact

Taeyeon has gained recognition in South Korea as both the leader of K-pop girl group Girls' Generation and a successful solo artist.<ref></ref><ref></ref> Janelle Okwodu of *Vogue* described Taeyeon as "the Beyoncé" of Girls' Generation whose fashion styles are "constantly on the go."<ref name="vogue"></ref> Taeyeon topped Gallup Korea's list of the most popular K-pop idols of 2016;<ref></ref> she also appeared in the rankings in 2015 (at number three), 2017 (at number nine), and 2019 (at number seven).<ref></ref> Taeyeon has been featured on the *Forbes* Korea Power Celebrity list in 2016 (at number 32),<ref name="forbestop402016"></ref> 2018 (at number 16),<ref></ref> and 2019 (at number 23).<ref></ref> In celebration of Women's History Month of 2024, The Recording Academy named Taeyeon on the list as one of 15 inspiring K-pop soloists.<ref></ref> In June 2015, she became the first K-pop female celebrity to reach seven million followers on Instagram and, as of November 2023, has over 19 million followers.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref>

Taeyeon sold over one million physical albums between 2011 and 2021, the highest-selling South Korean female artist at the time.<ref></ref> She has influenced numerous K-pop artists, including Lovelyz's Jiae,<ref></ref> Melody Day's Chahee,<ref></ref> Cosmic Girls' Luda,<ref></ref> CLC's Elkie,<ref></ref> Sonamoo's High.D,<ref></ref> Baek A-yeon,<ref></ref> Mamamoo's Moonbyul,<ref></ref> and Heize.<ref></ref> Boy band BTS named Taeyeon an inspiration for their 2014 single "Boy In Luv".<ref></ref>

## Philanthropy
In July 2019, Taeyeon donated ₩100 million to the Korean Red Cross, to help for support women, children and teenagers from low-income families in her hometown in North Jeolla.<ref></ref> She also became the 132nd member of the "Red Cross Honors Club".<ref></ref><ref></ref> The Red Cross Society of North Jeolla Province planned to expand its support for health supplies for women, girls and adolescents by recommending three primary, middle, and high schools in the region as beneficiaries of Taeyeon's donation.<ref></ref>

In January 2023, Taeyeon donated ₩100 million to the Stump Foundation for Youth, to be allocated for the support of vulnerable adolescents who are under out-of-home care and adolescents whose protection ends after turning 18 and who are now prepared to take on the responsibility of self-sufficiency. Through this donation, she became the second member of "The Forest Club" following former Hana Financial Group's Chairman Kim Jeong-tae, which is a group of major donors created by the Stump Foundation for Youth.<ref></ref> In July 2023, The Stump Foundation for Youth revealed its 1st "Dream Site Project" had been completed with Taeyeon's donation. The project was to improve child and youth welfare facilities. With her donation, interior & exterior facilities were improved such as installing shower booths and replacing tiles for women's bathroom, replacing stairs and railings for dormitory, replacing library decks, and installing air conditioners.<ref></ref>

In March 2025, Taeyeon donated ₩100 million to the Korean Red Cross to help victims recover from the March 2025 South Korea wildfires.<ref></ref>

## Endorsements
In Asia, Taeyeon has been regarded by some media outlets as a beauty icon and leader in fashion.<ref></ref> She has endorsed various Korean beauty brands since her debut, appearing in commercials and advertisements both as a solo artist and with the members of Girls' Generation and Girls' Generation-TTS. Taeyeon's first solo endorsement was of A-Solution, a brand of acne cosmetics, in 2008.<ref></ref> Two years later, she became the first female Asian brand ambassador of Korean skincare brand Nature Republic, along with boy group and labelmate Exo, for three consecutive years.<ref></ref> In 2016, when Taeyeon's contract with Nature Republic expired, the cosmetic brand Banila Co. selected her to be its new spokesmodel due to her domestic and international popularity.<ref></ref> In March 2019, Taeyeon represented the Chanel's Rouge Coco Flash Lipstick collection through beauty pictorial in the April issue of *Elle Korea*.<ref></ref> In September 2019, French luxury makeup brand Givenchy Beauty released a lipstick makeup pictorial with Taeyeon in magazine *High Cut* Vol. 247.<ref></ref> In May 2020, Taeyeon become a muse for A'pieu, a Korean beauty subsidiary brand of Missha; its official brand representative stated that "Taeyeon's pure atmosphere matches well to A'pieu's cosmetics."<ref></ref> In August 2021, she was selected as ambassador for South Korean super-premium skincare beauty brand Sulwhasoo's "New Concentrated Ginseng Renewing EX" campaign.<ref></ref> Amorepacific Corporation said that thanks to choosing Taeyeon to be the ambassador for this campaign, the "New Concentrated Ginseng Renewing EX" product line in the Chinese market contributed to a 50% growth in total Sulwhasoo sales and this product line has also grown strongly in the ASEAN region market.<ref></ref> Following the announcement, she promoted the Sulwhasoo's campaign in various magazines, including *Elle Korea*,<ref></ref> *Harper's Bazaar Korea*<ref></ref> and *Cosmopolitan Korea*.<ref></ref> In April 2022, Taeyeon was selected as a muse of global makeup brand Benefit Cosmetics.<ref></ref><ref></ref> In March 2023, Benefit Cosmetics had promoted her to its brand ambassador.<ref></ref> In the *Singles Korea* October 2023 issue, for which she was the cover girl, Taeyeon represented the Benefit Cosmetics' 24 Tint collection.<ref></ref> In March 2024, Benefit Cosmetics released a beauty pictorial with Taeyeon to commemorate the cumulative sales of 45 million units of "Benefit Cosmetics' Dandelion Blush".<ref></ref> In the *W Korea* February 2024 issue, Taeyeon cooperated with British high-end fragrance brand Penhaligon's to present its two perfume lines, "Penhaligon's Artemisia Eau de Parfum" and "Penhaligon's Luna Eau de Toilette".<ref></ref>

## Awards and nominations

Since debuting as a member of Girls' Generation, Taeyeon has received numerous nominations and awards for her solo activities in original soundtracks, radio show hosting and musical theatre. She won two Best Female Artist awards at the Mnet Asian Music Awards, as well as Bonsang awards at the 25th Seoul Music Awards and the 30th Golden Disc Awards.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref> In January 2020, Taeyeon won the OST Award, Bonsang Award (Main Prize), and Daesang Award (Grand Prize) at the 29th Seoul Music Awards.<ref></ref>
