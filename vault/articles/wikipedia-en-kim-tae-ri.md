**Kim Tae-ri** (; born April 24, 1990) is a South Korean actress. Known for her work in both television and film, she has received various accolades, including three Baeksang Arts Awards, one Blue Dragon Film Award and one Buil Film Award, in addition to nominations at the Grand Bell Awards and Chunsa Film Art Awards. 

Kim gained widespread recognition for her breakthrough role as Sook-hee in Park Chan-wook's critically acclaimed film *The Handmaiden* (2016), which premiered at the Cannes Film Festival and earned her the Blue Dragon Film Award for Best New Actress. She subsequently appeared in the political thriller *1987: When the Day Comes* (2017) and the drama film *Little Forest* (2018), which earned her Best Actress nominations at the Baeksang Arts Awards and the Blue Dragon Film Awards.

On television, Kim received the Baeksang Arts Award for Best Actress for her performances in *Twenty-Five Twenty-One* (2022) and *Jeongnyeon: The Star Is Born* (2024),<ref name="CHOSUNBIZ"></ref> and earned additional critical praise for *Mr. Sunshine* (2018) and *Revenant* (2023). In 2019, Kim was featured by *Forbes* in their 30 Under 30 list in the Entertainment & Sports category,<ref name="forbes"></ref><ref name=":1"></ref> and in 2024, Gallup Korea ranked her as Korea's Television Actor of the Year.<ref name=":2"></ref>

## Early life
Kim was born on April 24, 1990, in Sangbong-dong, Jungnang District, Seoul, South Korea.<ref name="Tae-ri" /> After graduating from Youngshin Nursing Business High School, she studied Journalism and Communication at Kyung Hee University from 2008 to 2012.<ref name="yonhap16"></ref><ref></ref> She was inspired to become an actress in her second year of university after joining a theater club.<ref></ref>

## Career
###  Early work and breakthrough (2012–2017) 

After graduating from university, Kim spent a year working as part of the technical crew for the Iru theatre troupe in Daehangno.<ref></ref> She was later cast as the understudy for Kang Ae-shim in the 2012 production of *Spoonface Steinberg,* although she did not perform on stage.<ref name=":0"></ref> She went on to appear in the plays *Pansy* and *Ask for Love*, and was double-cast in the 2013 rerun of *Spoonface Steinberg*.<ref name=":0" /><ref name="KOFIC"></ref> That same year, she filmed *Moon Young*, a short film that premiered at the Seoul Independent Film Festival in 2015, with a theatrical version released in 2017.<ref></ref><ref></ref> In 2014, Kim signed with J-Wide Company and began appearing in various television commercials.<ref></ref> During this period, she continued acting in short films and auditioned extensively for feature film roles, but faced numerous rejections.<ref name="KOFIC" />

In 2016, Kim made her feature film debut in Park Chan-wook's *The Handmaiden,* having been selected from among 1,500 actresses who auditioned for the role.<ref name="handmaiden"></ref> Her performance earned her multiple Best New Actress awards, including at the Blue Dragon Film Awards, Director's Cut Awards, Buil Film Awards, and Busan Film Critics Awards.<ref></ref> Park Chan-wook later remarked that his first impression of Kim strongly reminded him of his initial meeting with actress Kang Hye-jung, who achieved her breakthrough in his film *Oldboy* (2003).*<ref></ref><ref></ref>*

The following year, Kim appeared in the ensemble political thriller *1987: When the Day Comes**,* which earned her Best Actress nominations at the Grand Bell Awards and Chunsa Film Art Awards.<ref name="1987w"></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref>

###  Established actress (2018–present) 
In 2018, Kim headlined the Korean film adaptation of the manga series *Little Forest*, alongside Ryu Jun-yeol and Jin Ki-joo.<ref name="littleforest"></ref><ref></ref> Her performance won her a Director's Cut Award and garnered Best Actress nominations at the Blue Dragon Film Awards, Baeksang Arts Awards and Buil Film Awards.<ref name="54BAA" /><ref name="39BDFA" /> The same year, she made her television debut in the period melodrama *Mr. Sunshine,* written by Kim Eun-sook,*<ref name="mrsunshine"></ref>* earning her a third nomination at the Baeksang Arts Awards, this time for Best Actress in Television.<ref name="55BAA" /> Upon its final broadcast, *Mr. Sunshine* became the third highest-rated series in Korean cable television history.<ref></ref>

In 2019, Kim was named to Forbes 30 Under 30 list in the Entertainment & Sports category.<ref name="forbes" /><ref name=":1" />

In 2021, Kim starred as Captain Jang in Jo Sung-hee's *Space Sweepers*, regarded as the first Korean space blockbuster.<ref name="spacesweepers"></ref><ref></ref> Kim was drawn to the film due to its genre and her character's role, as she said: "I don't think there has ever been a [Korean] film in which a woman was a captain."<ref name="spacesweepers" /> After its release on Netflix,<ref></ref> the film received generally positive reviews, complimenting the characters and the special effects.<ref></ref> In a review for *Decider*, Johnny Loftus described Kim's performance as the "picture of laconic cool" and likened her interpretation of the character's selfishness and greediness to Tessa Thompson's Valkyrie in *Thor: Ragnarok* (2017).<ref></ref>

In July 2021, Kim moved to Management MMM after her contract with J-Wide expired.<ref></ref>

She returned to television in 2022, starring in the coming-of-age romance series *Twenty-Five Twenty-One*,<ref name="twentyfivetwentyone"></ref><ref></ref> which marked her second collaboration with one of the directors of *Mr. Sunshine*, Jung Ji-hyun.<ref name="twentyfivetwentyone2"></ref> Upon reading the script, Kim was drawn to the "bright, energetic nature" of her character Na Hee-do, a high school fencer overcoming difficulties during the IMF crisis.<ref name="twentyfivetwentyone2" /><ref name="twentyfivetwentyone" /> In preparation for the role, she learned fencing for six months, watched numerous YouTube videos and requested an expert on set during fencing scenes.<ref></ref> At the time, *Twenty-Five Twenty-One* was the 18th highest-rated series in Korean cable television history and achieved success on Netflix, leading to Kim's rise in popularity among international audiences.<ref></ref><ref name="alienoid1"></ref> *Cine21*<nowiki/>'s Song Kyung-won deemed Kim vital to the success of the series.<ref></ref> For her portrayal of Na Hee-do, she was named both Best Actress in Television and Most Popular Actress at the Baeksang Arts Awards.<ref name="58thbaeksang"></ref>

Later that year, Kim starred as Lee Ahn in the first part of Choi Dong-hoon's two-part science fiction action film *Alienoid*, alongside *Little Forest* co-star Ryu Jun-yeol and Kim Woo-bin.<ref></ref><ref name="alienoid"></ref> Kim attended action school and learned gymnastics, various types of martial arts and shooting to prepare for the role.<ref></ref> *Alienoid* was shot simultaneously with its follow-up film for 13 months.<ref name="alienoid2"></ref><ref name="alienoid" /> The film was a box-office failure, earning $12 million worldwide against a budget of over  billion ($22.9 million).<ref></ref><ref></ref> It received mixed reviews in South Korea,<ref name="alienoid" /><ref name="alienoid1" /> with Kim Boram of Yonhap News Agency criticizing the story but appreciating Kim and Ryu's chemistry and action sequences.<ref></ref> International critics wrote more positive reviews,<ref></ref> often praising the film's ensemble cast.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref>

In 2023, Kim starred in *Revenant*, alongside Oh Jung-se and Hong Kyung. She was praised for her dual portrayal of Gu San-yeong and the malicious spirit within her, and received the Grand Prize at the SBS Drama Awards.<ref></ref> The following year, Kim starred as the titular character in the television adaptation of the webtoon *Jeong-nyeon*, written by Seo Yi-re and illustrated by Na Mon.<ref name="jeongnyeon"></ref> The artist said that she had initially used Kim's character in *The Handmaiden* as reference while drawing Jeong-nyeon.<ref></ref> Kim's performance was widely praised for its emotional depth and vocal versatility,<ref></ref><ref></ref> earning her a second Baeksang Arts Award for Best Actress in Television.<ref></ref> Gallup Korea later ranked her as Korea's Television Actor of the Year.<ref name=":2" /> She also reprised her role as Lee Ahn in *Alienoid: Return to the Future*. Despite mixed reviews,<ref name="score:rotten"></ref> it opened at first place on Korean box office with 103,467 admissions and a gross of US$746,073.<ref></ref><ref></ref> 

Kim made her voice acting debut in the 2025 South Korean animated science fiction romance film *Lost in Starlight*, which was the first South Korean feature-length animated film to be released on Netflix.<ref></ref>

In 2026, Kim appeared as a regular cast member in *Curtain Up, Class!*, a new variety show produced by tvN.<ref></ref>

## Endorsements
 in 2023]]

Since 2017, Kim has served as a brand ambassador for the South Korean high-end cosmetics brand O Hui.<ref></ref> In 2018, she was chosen as face of Kenzo's signature fragrance, *Flower by Kenzo*, and was also appointed the Korean ambassador for the American luxury jewelry house Tiffany & Co., a role she held until 2021.<ref></ref><ref></ref> In 2021, she became a brand ambassador for Italian luxury fashion house Prada.<ref></ref> In January 2022, the South Korean women's fashion brand Itmichaa announced her as its new muse.<ref></ref>
