**Park Soo-young** (; born July 31, 1992),<ref></ref> is a South Korean singer and actress. She debuted under the stage name **Lizzy** () as a member of the South Korean girl group After School in March 2010 and later formed part of the group's sub-units, Orange Caramel and After School Blue. She made her solo debut in January 2015 with the digital single "Not an Easy Girl". Outside of her music career, Park has starred in various television dramas, including *All My Love* (2011) and *Angry Mom* (2015).

In May 2018, Park graduated from After School following the expiration of her contract, but remains a member of Orange Caramel.<ref name=graduated /><ref></ref> Following her departure, she shifted her work to acting and changed her stage name to **Park Soo-ah** ().<ref name=psa /> In November 2020, she changed her professional name, for a second time, to Park Soo-young, her birth name.<ref name=stagename2 />

## Early life and education
Park was born on July 31, 1992, in Busan, South Korea.<ref name="Russell2014"></ref> She attended Kyung Hee University, majoring in Post Modern Music.<ref></ref>

## Career

### 2009–2010: Career beginnings
Park served as Son Dam-bi's backup dancer in 2009. She was first hinted to be joining After School when she joined the members on stage to perform a cover of Fin.K.L.'s "To My Boyfriend" at their first fan meeting event in 2010, while donning a mask to keep her identity a secret. Park made her official debut as a member of After School under the stage name "Lizzy" when they released their third single "Bang!" on March 25, 2010. She was the youngest member of the group until E-Young joined the group in December 2010.<ref name="autogenerated1"></ref> In June 2010, Lizzy and her fellow After School members Raina and Nana formed a sub-unit named Orange Caramel and released their first mini-album featuring the lead single "Magic Girl".

### 2011–2017: Television roles and solo debut

In 2011, Park was briefly a cast member of the SBS variety show, *Running Man*. That same year, she made her acting debut as a supporting character on MBC's sitcom, *All My Love*.<ref></ref> Her appearances in *Running Man* were often edited out, and she eventually left to focus on *All My Love*.<ref></ref> In November 2012, Park was cast in MBC's weekend drama *Rascal Sons*, where she had a more significant role.<ref name=son></ref>

In December 2012, Park and indie rapper Andup released a collaborative single, "Cosmetic", for their single album *Cupcake Project*.<ref></ref> In the same month, Park joined Sistar's Bora, 4Minute's Gayoon, Secret's Sunhwa and Kara's Jiyoung to form the project girl group Mystic White for the 2012 *SBS Gayo Daejun*. They performed their single "Mermaid Princess" on the show, and the song's profits were donated to people in need.<ref></ref>

In July 2013, Park made a cameo appearance in the Japanese horror drama *Evil Spirit Ward*, playing the role of a Korean foreign student named Tehi.<ref name=horrordrama></ref> In 2014, she played a hair stylist named Hyeni in the film *Momo Salon.* The film consisted of six ten-minute episodes, and was released on Naver TV Cast on September 2.<ref name=momosalon></ref>

In December 2014, Park was cast as a member of the second season of Jeong Hyeong-don and Defconn's show, *Hitmaker*.<ref></ref> Lizzy, G.NA, 4Minute's Kwon So-hyun and Kara's Heo Young-ji became a girl group called Chamsonyeo () for the show.<ref></ref> The show began airing on January 16, 2015. She also had a supporting role in the film *Love Forecast*, which was released on January 15, 2015.<ref name="jumpingacting"></ref> That same month, Park replaced Kim Sung-eun as a co-MC of the food variety show, *Tasty Road*.<ref></ref>

On January 23, 2015, Park debuted as a solo singer with the trot song "Not An Easy Girl" ().<ref></ref> The music video used footage from the 1961 film adaptation of *Chunhyangga*, with Park playing the protagonist.<ref></ref> On February 11, she released a duet song, "Goodbye PMS", with comedian Park Myung-soo.<ref></ref> The song was written by Duble Sidekick, who said PMS (which usually means premenstrual syndrome) refers to "a monster that creates all the worries and stress suffered by a high school girl".<ref></ref> Park also had a supporting role in MBC's drama *Angry Mom*, which aired from March to May.<ref></ref><ref name="hello"></ref>

In 2017, she joined as main MC on the first season of  *My Daughter's Men*, being the only woman in the cast.<ref name="신현준X이수근X리지, '내딸의남자들' 3MC 확정 공식"></ref><ref></ref>

### 2018–present: Acting career and legal troubles
On May 1, 2018, Park graduated from After School following the expiration of her contract.<ref name=graduated></ref> On May 10, Park joined Celltrion Entertainment.<ref name="agency"></ref> On July 3 it was announced that Park had changed her promotional name from 'Lizzy' to 'Park Soo-ah',<ref name="psa"></ref> and was cast in the web drama *I Picked Up a Celebrity on the Street*.<ref name="web"></ref> On September 12,  it was revealed by  Celltrion Entertainment that Park had joined the main cast of the upcoming SBS drama *Fates & Furies* alongside Lee Min-jung, Joo Sang-wook, So Yi-hyun and Lee Ki-woo. The drama began to air on December 1.<ref></ref><ref></ref> She also had a cameo in *Devilish Charm*.

In 2019, she was cast in the app drama, *Kim Seul Gi Genius*, which was released in May 2019.<ref></ref> She was also part of the tvN's comedy drama, *Ugly Miss Young-ae 17*.<ref></ref>

In an interview and pictorial with @star1 magazine for their December 2020 issue, Park revealed that she would now be promoting herself under her birth name, Park Soo-young.<ref name="stagename2"></ref>

On May 18, 2021, Park hit a taxi with her car while driving in Cheongdam-dong, a neighborhood of Seoul. Upon the arrival of law enforcement at the scene, she was determined to have a blood alcohol content of 0.08. As a result, she was charged with a DUI and had her driver's license revoked.<ref></ref> In August 2021, Celltrion Entertainment announced that Park Soo-Ah's contract had expired, and after coming to an agreement between both parties, it would not be renewed.<ref></ref>

In October 2022, Park signed with new agency BK Entertainment.<ref></ref>
