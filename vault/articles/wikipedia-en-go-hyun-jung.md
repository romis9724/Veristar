**Go Hyun-jung** (; born March 2, 1971) is a South Korean actress and beauty pageant titleholder. She debuted in the entertainment industry as a Miss Korea runner-up in 1989 and went on to star in *Sandglass *(1995), one of the highest-rated and critically acclaimed dramas in Korean television history. She retired after marrying chaebol Chung Yong-jin in 1995, then returned to acting after their divorce in 2003. Go has since regained her top star status in Korea, becoming the highest-paid actress on TV after the success of her series' *Queen Seondeok* (2009) and *Big Thing* (2010).<ref name=10line>"10LINE: Ko Hyun-jung" . 10Asia. November 3, 2009.</ref>

## Life and career
### Early career and retirement
Go Hyun-jung graduated from Dongguk University with a degree in Performing Arts. She was a runner-up in the Miss Korea pageant in 1989, which launched her acting career.<ref></ref> Her big break came in the 1995 SBS drama *Sandglass*. The drama dealt with modern Korean history from 1970 to the 1990s and was one of the highest rated dramas in Korean television history, with average ratings of 50.8% and a peak of 64.5%.<ref></ref> People would rush home just to see a new episode, saying "it's time to go home," which meant they had to go home to watch *Sandglass*.<ref name="daemul interview">. Korea JoongAng Daily. November 4, 2010</ref> However, following her marriage, Go announced her retirement from the industry.<ref name="kt1" />

### Television comeback and films
She staged a comeback in the 2005 melodrama *Spring Day*,<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref> which she followed up with May–December romantic comedy *What's Up Fox?* and police procedural *H.I.T*.<ref></ref>

Not content with her small screen success, Go began her late-blooming movie career by going against her image and taking pay cuts to star in non-mainstream films.<ref></ref> She was part of the ensemble cast in arthouse films *Woman on the Beach* and *Like You Know It All* by auteur Hong Sang-soo.<ref></ref><ref>"해변의 연인 (The Woman on The Beach) Presentation Report". Twitch Film. April 17, 2006.</ref><ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref> She then starred in *Actresses*, a semi-improvisational movie featuring six actresses each playing themselves. Director E J-yong, who was first inspired to make the film after going out for a drink with actresses Youn Yuh-jung and Go in 2007, said he focused on conveying the "reality" of the actresses' lives.<ref>"Fact and fiction merge as South Korea's leading ladies come together" . Yonhap. December 1, 2009.</ref><ref>"*Actresses* is a miracle achieved, says Koh (Part 1)" . 10Asia. November 23, 2009.</ref><ref>"*Actresses* is a miracle achieved, says Koh (Part 2)" . 10Asia. November 23, 2009.</ref>

### Career resurgence
Go regained her reputation as Korea's top actress in 2009 after playing femme fatale  royal concubine Lady Mishil in the hit historical drama *Queen Seondeok*.<ref></ref><ref name=changes>"Ko Hyun-jung Talks About Changes On Screen and Off" . The Chosun Ilbo. June 6, 2009.</ref> She was not the titular lead character, but her interpretation of the power-hungry main rival to the queen was impressive enough to make viewers remember the show as Mishil's story.<ref name="daemul interview" /><ref>"Supporting Roles Stealing Spotlight" . The Korea Times. August 3, 2009.</ref><ref>"10 Awards - Top 10 People of 2009" . 10Asia. December 23, 2009.</ref> The drama reached ratings of over 40 percent and won her the highest award at the MBC Drama Awards and Baeksang Arts Awards.<ref>"Ko Hyun-jung Wins Top Drama Award" . The Korea Times. December 31, 2009.</ref><ref>"Ko Hyun-jung, *Haeundae* win grand prize at PaekSang Arts Awards" . 10Asia. March 29, 2010.</ref> Go was named Gallup Korea's Television Actor of the Year for two consecutive years (2009–10).<ref></ref>

That same year, Go surprised fans not only by appearing on a TV entertainment show (popular talk show *Golden Fishery* hosted by comedian Kang Ho-dong) for the first time in 15 years, but also by frankly addressing rumors surrounding her personal life. Once notorious for declining to appear on entertainment programs other than dramas or films, she maintained a mysterious image for a long time. Her new easy-going, and down-to-earth attitude seemingly reflected a philosophical view of the many ups and downs in life. In interviews with print and online magazines, Go has expressed her feelings towards her ex-husband and children.<ref name="changes" /><ref>"Actress Ko Reveals Truth About Rumors" . The Korea Times. January 22, 2009.</ref><ref></ref>

Twenty-one years after making her acting debut, Go held her very first fanmeeting on June 13, 2010—she held a press conference beforehand, sang songs for the 500 fans present, answered questions, and prepared video clips.<ref></ref>

Though plagued with production issues prior to airing,<ref>"Producers Sue Ko Hyun-jung Over Unmade Soap" . The Chosun Ilbo. August 21, 2009.</ref><ref>"Stars Sued for Contract Breach" . The Korea Times. August 21, 2009.</ref> Go returned to television ten months later in *Daemul*, which means "big shot" or "big thing" in Korean.<ref>"Ko Hyun-joung to turn 1st female president in new drama" . 10Asia. December 31, 2009.</ref><ref>"Actress Ko to Return as President in New Drama" . The Korea Times. March 17, 2008.</ref> In the drama, Go plays Seo Hye-rim, an anchorwoman who enters politics after the death of her war correspondent husband and becomes the nation's first female president. The 24-episode series revolves around an ordinary woman in extraordinary circumstances: how and why she became head of state and also the complicated schemes and plots surrounding her, her presidency, allies, and enemies.<ref>"Will female president appeal to TV drama fans?" . The Korea Times. October 5, 2010.</ref> Besides its star-studded cast (Go starred opposite Korean Wave star Kwon Sang-woo), the show's ratings was helped by its controversial plot, which included events from Korea's recent past such as a presidential impeachment and the sinking of a Navy warship, and it topped its time slot for 11 consecutive weeks.<ref name="daemul interview" /><ref>"SBS *The President* ends run on top for 11th consecutive week" . 10Asia December 24, 2010.</ref> Go repeated her feat by winning the top prize at the 2010 SBS Drama Awards.<ref name=sbs2010>"Ko Hyun-jung wins grand prize at SBS Drama Awards" . 10Asia. January 3, 2011.</ref> According to industry sources, Go was reportedly paid  () per episode, setting a new salary record for a Korean actress.<ref>"Koh Hyun-jung, highest paid Korean actress" . The Korea Times. April 12, 2011.</ref><ref>"Ko Hyun-jung Sets New Soap Opera Earnings Record" . The Chosun Ilbo. April 12, 2011.</ref>

Go also narrated the SBS documentary *The Last Tundra - Movie Edition* (also known as *The Final Tundra - Cinema Edition*) which offered a rare glimpse into the life of the Nenets, the last reindeer herding nomads living in the Siberian tundra.<ref>"Documentary *The Last Tundra* shows culture's respect for nature". The Korea Herald. February 13, 2011.</ref>

2012's *Miss Go* (international title: *Miss Conspirator*) was her first commercial film, and the first film she headlined.<ref></ref> The action comedy is about a nerdy, reclusive cartoonist with a severe case of sociophobia who somehow gets mixed up in a drug deal involving one of the biggest organized crime groups in Korea, and is forced to deal with her phobia and interact with others as she runs from the police.<ref>"Ko to return as reclusive cartoonist" . The Korea Herald. May 29, 2012.</ref><ref>. Korea JoongAng Daily. June 1, 2012.</ref><ref>. StarN News. June 5, 2012.</ref><ref></ref><ref>. Korea JoongAng Daily. June 15, 2012.</ref>

Go returned to television in 2013 with *The Queen's Classroom*, a remake of the 2005 Japanese drama *Jyoou no Kyoushitsu*. The story depicts the struggle between a ferocious and ruthless elementary school teacher and her class.<ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref><ref></ref>

In 2016, Go starred in the family melodrama *Dear My Friends*,<ref></ref> playing a freelance translator, a complex and sometimes abrasive character, who observes the lives of the old people surrounding her.<ref></ref>

In 2017, Go announced her return to the big screen with *A Winter Guest Scarier than a Tiger*, by acclaimed indie filmmaker Lee Kwang-kuk.<ref></ref>

In 2018, Go starred in the legal thriller drama *Return*.<ref></ref> The drama revolves around a murder case that involves powerful members of an elite class, with Go playing the role of a rags-to-riches lawyer with secrets.<ref></ref> However halfway through the series, it was announced that she dropped out due to irreconcilable differences with the PD.<ref></ref>

In early 2019, Go starred in the second season of *My Lawyer, Mr. Jo*, alongside Park Shin-yang.<ref></ref>

In 2021, more than two years after her last project, Go returned to the small-screen with *Reflection of You*, a JTBC melodrama based on a novel by Jung So-hyeon.<ref></ref>

## Other activities
In 2010, after her contract ended with De Chocolate E&TF, she set up her own talent agency with her brother, Go Byung-cheol, as CEO, called IOK Company.<ref></ref> In 2012, her *Spring Day* co-star and close friend, Zo In-sung, also signed on.<ref></ref>

She published a book on skincare titled *Go Hyun-jung's Texture* in 2011. The actress has always been admired for her youthful appearance, largely attributed to her healthy skin which had made fans wonder about her beauty secrets. The book, arranged in the format of a documentary, contains Go's own philosophy on beauty classified under six themes—texture, color, light, line, formality, and scent—as well as her ideas on leading a sound and healthy lifestyle in general. The book is co-written by another writer who observed the actress's everyday life for six months, describing it in detail.<ref>. Korea JoongAng Daily. April 27, 2011.</ref><ref>"Actress Ko Hyun-jung publishes book on skin care" . 10Asia. April 25, 2011.</ref> The book became a bestseller, with all 30,000 copies of the first edition selling out in just two days of release.<ref>"Actress Ko Hyun-jung's skincare book selling fast" . 10Asia. May 3, 2011.</ref>

At the press event for her eponymously named talk show *GO Show* (which premiered on April 6, 2012), Go quipped, "I became an MC because I wanted to. I want to meet a lot of people and hear their stories. I have always wanted to do that and SBS gave me that opportunity."<ref name="cakewalk" /> Given her reputation for saying what is on her mind (one that has won the actress many fans), concerns arose as to how her work as show host would affect the impressive image of flawless elegance and sophistication she has gained over the years. But Go harbored little anxiety over how her public image might change, saying, "I think I can afford to put a small dent in my image and have some fun."<ref name=cakewalk>"Ko Hyeon-jeong says hosting own show not a cakewalk" . The Korea Herald. March 28, 2012.</ref> Co-hosted by singer-songwriter Yoon Jong-shin, and comedians Jung Hyung-don and Kim Young-chul, the talk show was cancelled after eight months on the air.<ref></ref>

In 2014, she began teaching acting at her alma mater Dongguk University, as an adjunct professor in the theater department.<ref></ref> Back in 2006, she donated a  scholarship fund to the school.<ref></ref>

Go also launched her own fashion brand, atti.k, a label that sells clothes, shoes, and bags.<ref></ref> In the following year, Go then launched her own cosmetics line, koY.<ref></ref>

In 2015, Go starred in her own reality show, which tracks her trip to Tokyo and films her preparation to launch a travel book.<ref></ref>

## Personal life
In May 1995, at the peak of her career, Go married Chung Yong-jin, vice chairman and CEO of Shinsegae Group and grandson of Samsung founder Lee Byung-chul. The two first met in New York, purely by coincidence, when Chung had offered to help Go find her seat at the Winter Garden Theatre because her English was so bad.<ref>. Korea JoongAng Daily. April 24, 2007.</ref> After the most talked-about wedding in Korea at the time, Go announced her retirement from acting to focus on her new role as a chaebol's wife.<ref name="kt1">"Actress Ko Reveals Truth About Rumors" . The Korea Times. January 22, 2009.</ref> They had two children together, Haechan (born in 1998) and Haein (born in 2000).<ref></ref>

After eight years of marriage, a messy divorce followed in November 2003, during which she lost complete custody of her children.<ref name="daemul interview" /><ref></ref> Two years after the divorce, Shinsegae passed down a ruling stating that none of its department stores would be allowed to display any Go-related material, including any of her product endorsements.<ref></ref>
